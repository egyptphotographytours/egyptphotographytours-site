Egypt's Mediterranean Coast Beyond Alexandria

Discover Marsa Matruh's turquoise waters, El Alamein's historical sites, and hidden beaches. Your ultimate 2026 guide to coastal photography and sustainable travel along the North Coast.

Home/ Resources/ Mediterranean Coast Guide 2026
Updated June 20, 2026 35 min read Coastal Travel By Hossam, Egyptologist & Photographer

Egypt is synonymous with the Nile, the Pyramids, and the Red Sea. Yet, stretching over 1,000 kilometers along the country's northern border lies a completely different world: the Mediterranean Coast. Beyond the cosmopolitan charm of Alexandria, the North Coast reveals dramatic limestone cliffs, blindingly white sandbars, and waters that shift from sapphire to turquoise. For photographers and history enthusiasts, this region offers a compelling juxtaposition of pristine natural beauty and profound WWII historical sites. We will navigate the bustling local beaches of Marsa Matruh, pay solemn respects at the memorials of El Alamein, and seek out the hidden, untouched coves that few tourists ever see. Whether you are looking to capture the perfect seascape or understand the historical weight of the North African campaign, Egypt Photography Tours provides the expert guidance you need to explore this fascinating region responsibly and beautifully.

Chapter 1: Marsa Matruh – The Pearl of the Mediterranean

Located roughly 240 kilometers west of Alexandria, Marsa Matruh is the gateway to the western Mediterranean coast. Unlike the crowded summer resorts closer to the Delta, Matruh retains a rugged, authentic charm. The water here is famously clear, protected by natural rock formations that create calm, swimming-friendly lagoons.

Agiba Beach
Meaning "Wonder" or "Miracle," Agiba features dramatic limestone cliffs that plunge into crystal-clear waters. A paradise for landscape and seascape photographers.
Cleopatra Beach
Legend says Cleopatra bathed here. Surrounded by palm trees and soft white sand, it offers a serene setting for golden hour portraits and cultural storytelling.
Rommel Beach
Named after the WWII field marshal who used a nearby cave as his headquarters. It combines historical intrigue with stunning coastal vistas.

For those interested in the maritime history of the region, exploring the old fishing harbor of Matruh provides incredible documentary opportunities. The traditional wooden boats and the daily catch offer a glimpse into the enduring lifestyle of the local Bedouin and coastal communities. To understand more about sailing and coastal navigation in Egypt, read our North Coast Sailing Guide.

Chapter 2: El Alamein – History Etched in Sand and Stone

Just east of Marsa Matruh lies El Alamein, a name forever etched in the annals of military history. The site of two pivotal World War II battles in 1942, the El Alamein campaign turned the tide in the North African theater. Today, the coastline is dotted with cemeteries, museums, and memorials that serve as poignant reminders of the cost of war.

Photography Etiquette at Historical Sites

When photographing the Commonwealth War Cemetery or the El Alamein Military Museum, respect is paramount. Maintain a solemn demeanor, avoid using flash in indoor exhibition spaces, and use a telephoto lens to capture intricate details on gravestones without intruding on the space. For a deep dive into the history, see our El Alamein WWII Battlefields Guide.

Key Historical Landmarks

  • The Commonwealth War Cemetery: A beautifully maintained garden of remembrance containing over 7,000 graves. The uniformity of the white headstones against the green lawn and blue sky creates a powerful, minimalist photographic composition.
  • El Alamein Military Museum: Houses artifacts, uniforms, and maps from the battle. Great for capturing the textures of history.
  • The Italian and German Memorials: Distinctive architectural styles that reflect the nations they honor, offering striking contrasts against the desert backdrop.

Chapter 3: Hidden Beaches & The Rise of Eco-Tourism

Between the major towns, the North Coast is fractured into a series of hidden coves, salt flats, and untouched sandbars. As mass tourism develops along the eastern Delta coast, the western stretch remains a haven for those seeking solitude. Sustainable travel is crucial here to protect the fragile coastal ecosystems. Learn more about responsible travel in our Sustainable Travel & Eco-Lodges Guide and Plastic-Free Travel Guide.

Leave No Trace on the Mediterranean

The Mediterranean coast is vulnerable to pollution and plastic waste. When visiting hidden beaches, ensure you pack out all your trash. Egypt Photography Tours actively organizes and participates in coastal clean-up initiatives, ensuring that the pristine landscapes we photograph remain protected for future generations.

Chapter 4: Coastal Photography Masterclass

Capturing the Mediterranean requires specific techniques to handle the intense light, the reflective water, and the historical weight of the sites. Ensure your gear is protected from fine sand and salt spray by reviewing our Photography Gear Guide.

  • Polarizing Filters are Mandatory: The sun reflecting off the turquoise water can blow out your highlights. A circular polarizer (CPL) will cut the glare, deepen the blue of the sky, and reveal the underwater sandbars.
  • Chase the Blue Hour: The 30 minutes after sunset at El Alamein offers a melancholic, ethereal light that perfectly matches the reflective nature of the memorials.
  • Drone Regulations: Egypt has strict drone laws. Flying without a permit is illegal and can result in confiscation. Always check our Egypt Drone Laws Guide before packing your quadcopter.
  • Leading Lines from the Cliffs: At Agiba Beach, use the jagged limestone formations as leading lines to draw the viewer's eye toward the horizon and the endless sea.

Frequently Asked Questions

What are the best beaches to visit on Egypt's Mediterranean coast beyond Alexandria?
Top recommendations include Agiba Beach in Marsa Matruh for dramatic cliffs and turquoise waters, Cleopatra Beach for its historical setting, and the pristine, untouched coves between Marsa Matruh and El Alamein. These locations offer minimal crowds and exceptional coastal photography opportunities.
Is it safe to travel to Egypt's North Coast and Marsa Matruh?
Yes, Egypt's Mediterranean coast, including Marsa Matruh and El Alamein, is very safe for tourists. The area is heavily frequented by domestic tourists during the summer and has a strong security presence. Traveling with a reputable guide ensures a smooth and secure experience.
What historical significance does El Alamein hold?
El Alamein was the site of two decisive WWII battles in 1942 that turned the tide in the North African campaign. Today, it features the Commonwealth War Cemetery, the El Alamein Military Museum, and various memorials, offering powerful documentary photography opportunities.
When is the best time to visit the Mediterranean coast of Egypt?
The best times are spring (March to May) and autumn (September to November) when temperatures are pleasant for exploring historical sites and beaches. Summer (June to August) is extremely hot but popular for swimming, while winter can be too cool and rainy for beach activities.
Does Egypt Photography Tours offer coastal photography extensions?
Yes, we offer specialized coastal photography guidance and can arrange private extensions to Marsa Matruh and El Alamein from Cairo or Alexandria. Our 7-Day Egypt Discovery Tour and private tours can be customized to include these stunning Mediterranean landscapes.
