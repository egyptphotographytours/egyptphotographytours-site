Chapter 2: e-Visa vs. Visa on Arrival - The Complete Breakdown

Price is just one factor. Let's dive deep into the nuances of each option.

Option A: The Egyptian e-Visa (The Planner's Choice)

The e-Visa is Egypt's official online portal, designed for travelers who prefer to have everything sorted before they leave home.

Who is it for?

  • Planners: You like to have your ducks in a row before a trip.
  • Peak Season Travelers: Avoid potentially long queues at Visa on Arrival banks during busy periods.
  • Multiple-Entry Needs: If you plan to leave Egypt (e.g., a side trip to Jordan) and come back, the $60 multiple-entry e-Visa is your only option.
  • Peace of Mind Seekers: Eliminate the "what if" of landing and facing an issue at the visa counter.

Step-by-Step e-Visa Application

1
Visit the Official Portal: Go to the official Egypt e-Visa website (visa2egypt.gov.eg). Be wary of third-party sites that charge hidden fees.
2
Create an Account: Sign up with your email address and verify it.
3
Fill the Application: Select "Tourism" and choose "Single" or "Multiple" entry. Enter your details exactly as they appear on your passport.
4
Upload Documents: You'll need a scan of your passport's bio page (under 1 MB, JPG or PDF). A passport photo may also be required.
5
Host Information: This is where a tour operator helps. You'll need to enter a "Host" (like our local partner). For tours with Egypt Photography Tours, we provide you with the exact details to use.
6
Pay and Wait: Pay with Visa or Mastercard. Processing typically takes 48-72 hours, but apply at least a week in advance to be safe. You'll receive your e-Visa via email as a PDF. Print it out and bring it with you!

Option B: Visa on Arrival (The Flexible Flyer's Choice)

The VOA is a straightforward process available at all major international airports in Egypt (Cairo, Luxor, Aswan, Hurghada, etc.) and some land borders.

Who is it for?

  • Last-Minute Travelers: You booked your trip with less than a week to go.
  • Spontaneous Types: You prefer to handle things as they come.
  • Travelers with Complex Nationalities: While most Western countries are eligible, it's always good to double-check.

Step-by-Step Visa on Arrival Process

1
Follow the Signs: After deplaning, follow the crowd towards "Immigration" and "Banking."
2
Find the Bank Counter: Before you reach immigration, you'll see one or more bank kiosks. This is where you purchase the visa sticker.
3
Pay the Fee: Hand over $30 USD in clean, crisp bills per person. Having the exact change is crucial, as tellers often have limited change. They will give you a sticker.
4
Proceed to Immigration: Take the sticker and your passport to the immigration officer. They will affix the sticker and stamp you in.

Critical VOA Warning

While the March 2026 fee is $30, Egypt is still using the old $25 visa stickers. To account for the $5 increase, an additional $5 sticker is affixed next to the original one. Don't be alarmed by this; it's the new official procedure. Just ensure you receive both stickers for your $30.

Option C: The "Sinai-Only" Stamp (The Wildcard)

If you are flying directly into Sharm El Sheikh, Dahab, Nuweiba, or Taba (popular Red Sea resorts), you can get a free 14-day entry stamp to the South Sinai region.

The Catch: This stamp strictly limits you to the South Sinai governorate. You cannot travel to Cairo, Luxor, or any other part of Egypt. If you plan to see the Pyramids or the Nile Valley, you must obtain a full national visa (e-Visa or VOA).

Chapter 5: Beyond the Visa - Non-Negotiable Entry Rules

Having a visa is just one part of the equation. Make sure you meet these requirements to avoid being turned away at the gate.

Passport Validity: The Six-Month Rule

Your passport must be valid for at least six months beyond your intended date of departure from Egypt. This is strictly enforced by airlines and immigration officials.

You also need at least one completely blank visa page (two is safer) for the entry stamp.

Required Documents to Carry

  • Return Flight Ticket: Proof of onward or return travel.
  • Hotel Reservations / Tour Itinerary: You may be asked where you are staying. Having your Egypt Photography Tours confirmation handy is perfect for this.
  • Sufficient Funds: While rarely asked for at the airport, it's good practice to be able to show you have means to support your stay.

Health Requirements

Currently, no COVID-19 documentation is required. A Yellow Fever vaccination certificate is mandatory only for travelers arriving from countries with a risk of yellow fever transmission.
