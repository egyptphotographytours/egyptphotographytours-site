Chapter 1: Cairo & Giza – The Eternal City (Days 1–4)

🏛️ Cairo & Giza: Where History Begins
Photography Potential 95%

Real Location Details

Cairo is a sprawling metropolis where ancient history meets modern chaos. The Giza Plateau, located on the outskirts, houses the last surviving Wonder of the Ancient World. The city is also home to the world's greatest collection of pharaonic antiquities.

Population: 20+ million | Key Sites: Pyramids, Sphinx, GEM, Islamic Cairo

Photography Highlights

  • Giza Plateau: Golden hour shots of the Pyramids and Sphinx.
  • Grand Egyptian Museum (GEM): Massive statues of Ramses II and Tutankhamun's full collection.
  • Khan el-Khalili: Vibrant market scenes, lanterns, and spice stalls.
  • Islamic Cairo: Stunning mosques and medieval architecture.
1

Day 1: Arrival & First Glimpses Arrival

Upon landing at Cairo International Airport, your private transfer will whisk you to your hotel. Choose between a stay in Giza with pyramid views or in Zamalek for a more laid-back vibe. In the evening, take a gentle stroll along the Nile Corniche or enjoy a welcome dinner.

Photography Tip: If you're in Giza, the sunset light on the pyramids from your hotel terrace is a perfect warm-up shot. See our pyramid view hotels guide.

2

Day 2: The Giza Plateau – Icons of Eternity Must-Do

Start early to beat the crowds. Your Egyptologist guide will lead you through the Great Pyramid of Khufu, Khafre, and Menkaure. Stand before the enigmatic Sphinx and visit the Solar Boat Museum. A camel ride to the panoramic viewpoint offers a classic photo opportunity.

Tour Integration: This is exactly how our Private Pyramids Photography Tour unfolds, with a professional photographer capturing you against these timeless backdrops.

3

Day 3: Museums & Bazaars – The Heart of Cairo Culture

Spend the morning at the Egyptian Museum in Tahrir Square or the new Grand Egyptian Museum (GEM). After lunch, dive into the labyrinthine alleys of Khan el-Khalili. Practice your haggling skills and enjoy mint tea at El Fishawy café.

Photography Tip: Use a wide-angle lens for the bustling market scenes. Check our ethical shopping guide.

4

Day 4: Coptic Cairo & Optional Saqqara History

Explore Coptic Cairo—the oldest part of the city. Visit the Hanging Church, the Coptic Museum, and the Ben Ezra Synagogue. Optional extension to Saqqara to see the Step Pyramid of Djoser and Dahshur for the Bent Pyramid.

In the evening, board the Watania Sleeper Train to Aswan. See our railway guide for tips.

Chapter 2: Nubian Gateway & The Nile – Aswan & Abu Simbel (Days 5–6)

🌴 Aswan: The Jewel of the South
Relaxation Level 90%

Real Location Details

Aswan is a beautiful, relaxed city on the first cataract of the Nile. It is the gateway to Nubian culture and the site of the High Dam, Lake Nasser, and the magnificent relocated temples of Philae and Abu Simbel.

Vibe: Relaxed, scenic, Nubian culture | Best Time: Late afternoon felucca sail

Photography Highlights

  • Philae Temple: Beautiful island setting, best in late afternoon light.
  • Abu Simbel: Colossal statues of Ramses II, dramatic desert setting.
  • Nubian Villages: Colorful houses, vibrant doors, and friendly locals.
  • Felucca Sail: Golden hour on the Nile with palm-lined banks.
5

Day 5: Aswan Arrival – Temples & Felucca Dreams Scenic

Wake up to the sight of palm trees and the Nile. Take a motorboat to Philae Temple, dedicated to the goddess Isis. Visit the Unfinished Obelisk in the granite quarries. In the late afternoon, experience a felucca sail around Elephantine Island as the sun sets.

Photography Tip: Bring a polarizing filter to deepen the blues of the water and sky. See our gear packing guide.

6

Day 6: Abu Simbel – Ramses II's Masterpiece Epic

An early start for the convoy to Abu Simbel. The three-hour drive through the desert is rewarded by the sight of the Great Temple of Ramses II and the smaller temple of Nefertari. Back in Aswan, visit a Nubian Village on the west bank.

Tour Integration: Our 7-Day Egypt Discovery Tour includes Abu Simbel and a Nubian Village visit, with all logistics handled.

Chapter 3: The Nile Cruise – Aswan to Luxor (Days 7–8)

🚢 Nile Cruise: The Quintessential Egyptian Experience
Historical Density 98%

Real Location Details

A 3-4 night Nile cruise between Aswan and Luxor is the most relaxing way to see ancient Egypt. You unpack once while floating past timeless river scenery, stopping at incredibly preserved temples along the way.

Duration: 3-4 Nights | Key Stops: Kom Ombo, Edfu, Esna Lock

Photography Highlights

  • Kom Ombo: Unique double temple dedicated to Sobek and Horus.
  • Edfu: Best-preserved temple in Egypt, massive pylons.
  • Esna Lock: Fascinating modern Nile engineering.
  • Sunrise on Deck: Mist over the Nile, farmers working the banks.
7

Day 7: Embarkation & Kom Ombo by Sunset Relaxing

Board your Nile cruise ship. After lunch, the ship sails north to Kom Ombo. Visit in the late afternoon when the low sun casts dramatic shadows across the reliefs. The onsite Crocodile Museum is a small but fascinating addition.

Pro Tip: For a detailed guide on these temples, see our Edfu & Kom Ombo Nile Cruise Guide.

8

Day 8: Edfu's Temple & the Esna Lock Majestic

Early morning arrival at Edfu. Disembark and take a horse-drawn carriage to the Temple of Horus. Its towering pylons and dark interior are incredibly atmospheric. Return to the cruise and sail toward Luxor, passing through the Esna Lock.

Photography Tip: Use a wide-angle lens inside the hypostyle hall to capture the massive scale of the columns.

Chapter 4: Luxor's East & West Bank (Days 9–10)

🏺 Luxor: The World's Greatest Open-Air Museum
Photography Potential 100%

Real Location Details

Ancient Thebes (modern Luxor) was the capital of Egypt during the New Kingdom. The East Bank was the domain of the living (massive temples), while the West Bank was the domain of the dead (royal tombs and mortuary temples).

Key Sites: Valley of the Kings, Karnak, Luxor Temple, Hatshepsut

Photography Highlights

  • Valley of the Kings: Vibrant tomb paintings (if permitted), dramatic desert valley.
  • Karnak Temple: The massive Hypostyle Hall, sacred lake, obelisks.
  • Hatshepsut Temple: Striking modernist architecture built into cliffs.
  • Hot Air Balloon: Sunrise flight over the West Bank tombs and temples.
9

Day 9: Luxor's West Bank – City of the Dead Ancient

Disembark in Luxor. Start with the Valley of the Kings, where pharaohs like Tutankhamun were buried. Next, marvel at the Temple of Hatshepsut at Deir el-Bahari. On the way back, stop at the Colossi of Memnon.

Optional Sunrise Magic: Book a hot-air balloon ride over the West Bank. See our balloon photography guide.

10

Day 10: Karnak & Luxor Temples – Then to the Coast Monumental

Spend the morning at the Karnak Temple Complex. The Hypostyle Hall is a photographer's dream. Afterwards, visit Luxor Temple in the heart of the city. In the early afternoon, drive 4-5 hours through the Eastern Desert to Hurghada on the Red Sea.

Pro Tip: For a deep dive into these temples, read our Karnak vs Luxor Temple Guide.

Chapter 5: Red Sea Relaxation (Days 11–12)

🌊 Red Sea: Crystal Waters & Coral Reefs
Relaxation Level 100%

Real Location Details

The Red Sea Riviera offers world-class snorkeling and diving. After days of temple-hopping, 3-4 days on the coast let you recharge on pristine beaches or beneath the waves. Hurghada is a popular resort town with excellent facilities.

Activities: Snorkeling, diving, desert safaris, resort relaxation

Photography Highlights

  • Underwater World: Vibrant coral gardens and technicolor fish.
  • Desert Safari: Quad biking or jeep safari into the Eastern Desert.
  • Marina Sunsets: Beautiful sunsets over the Red Sea marina.
11

Day 11: Red Sea – Snorkeling or Diving Marine Life

Book a boat trip to Giftun Island or one of the pristine offshore reefs. Snorkel among vibrant coral gardens. If you're a certified diver, the walls and drop-offs are legendary.

Photography Tip: If you have an underwater housing or a GoPro, this is the day to use it. See our Red Sea non-divers guide.

12

Day 12: Desert Safari or Second Day of Bliss Adventure

Take a quad bike or jeep safari into the Eastern Desert, visiting a Bedouin village and watching the sunset over the dunes. Alternatively, enjoy a second day of diving, or simply lounge by the pool.

Chapter 6: Return to Cairo & Departure (Days 13–14)

13

Day 13: Fly Back to Cairo Final Moments

A short one-hour flight brings you back to Cairo. Use this final afternoon for any missed sights—perhaps revisit the Egyptian Museum, or shop for last-moment souvenirs. Consider a farewell dinner on a Nile dinner cruise.

Tour Integration: Our Alexandria Day Trip can be added here if you have an extra day, but with 14 days, you'll want to save Alexandria for a future journey.

14

Day 14: Departure Farewell

After breakfast, your private transfer will take you to Cairo International Airport for your flight home, filled with thousands of images and indelible memories.

Photography Masterclass for Your 2-Week Trip

Capture Egypt at Its Finest

To capture Egypt at its finest, follow these pro tips from our team:

  • Gear Up: A 16-35mm wide-angle is essential for the Hypostyle Hall and giant statues. A 70-200mm telephoto lets you isolate details and compress the pyramid landscapes.
  • Golden Hours: Be at Karnak or Philae just before sunset. The low light warms the stone and creates long, dramatic shadows.
  • Filters Matter: A circular polarizer cuts through haze and deepens blue skies—vital for outdoor temple shots.
  • Respect Rules: No flash inside tombs. Tripods often require special permits; we handle that for you.
  • People First: Always ask before photographing locals. A small tip (baksheesh) is appreciated if you take a portrait.

For more equipment advice, see our photography gear packing guide and photography permits guide.

Frequently Asked Questions

What is the best 2-week itinerary for Egypt?
The ideal 2-week Egypt itinerary includes 3-4 days in Cairo (Pyramids, Museums), 3-4 days for a Nile Cruise from Aswan to Luxor, 2-3 days exploring Luxor's temples, and 3-4 days relaxing at the Red Sea. This balances ancient history, cultural immersion, and relaxation.
How many days do you need in Cairo?
For a 2-week trip, allocate at least 3 full days in Cairo. This covers the Giza Pyramids, the Egyptian Museum or GEM, Islamic Cairo, and Coptic Cairo, with time for a Saqqara excursion.
Is a Nile cruise worth it for a 2-week trip?
Absolutely. A 3-4 night Nile cruise between Aswan and Luxor is the quintessential Egyptian experience, allowing you to visit temples like Kom Ombo and Edfu effortlessly while relaxing. See our Nile Cruise Itinerary.
Can I combine the Red Sea with a 2-week itinerary?
Yes! After temple-hopping, 3-4 days in a Red Sea resort like Hurghada provides perfect relaxation. It's a 4-5 hour drive from Luxor, and you can fly back to Cairo from there.

Yes, we specialize in fully personalized Egypt itineraries based on your interests, photography goals, and travel style. Visit our Custom Tours page to start planning.
