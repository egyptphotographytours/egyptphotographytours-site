Chapter 5: Health, Safety & Nutrition - Keeping Your Family Well

üíß Water Safety Protocol

Drinking Water: Only bottled or purified water. Our tours provide unlimited sealed bottles.

Brushing Teeth: Use bottled water, not tap water.

Ice & Beverages: Only in established restaurants we've vetted.

Fruits & Vegetables: Peeled or cooked only. We provide sanitized fruit at meals.

Food Introduction Strategy for Kids

1

Days 1-2: Familiar Foundations

Begin with international hotel breakfasts, familiar pasta dishes, grilled chicken, and rice. Introduce mild Egyptian breads and simple desserts.

2

Days 3-5: Gentle Introductions

Try koshari (lentils, rice, pasta), tameya (Egyptian falafel), grilled kofta, and fresh fruit juices. Monitor reactions and adjust.

3

Days 6+: Adventurous Exploration

For willing children: molokhia soup, stuffed pigeon, traditional desserts. Always have familiar backup options available.

Frequently Asked Questions - Egypt Family Travel 2026

Q: What vaccinations do we need for Egypt with kids?

A: Standard travel vaccinations (Hepatitis A, Typhoid) are recommended. Consult your pediatrician 6-8 weeks before travel. No yellow fever vaccination required unless arriving from affected country.

Q: Can we find Western food for picky eaters?

A: Yes! Major hotels and tourist areas offer familiar options. We also provide snack packs and can arrange meals with specific dietary requirements.

Q: What's the best way to handle money with kids?

A: We recommend credit cards for hotels/tours, USD/EUR cash for markets (small bills), and Egyptian pounds for small purchases. Money belts for older children work well.

Q: Are there laundry services available?

A: Most hotels offer same-day laundry service (approximately $2-3 per item). We recommend packing light and planning one laundry day mid-trip.

Q: What about internet and communication?

A: Free Wi-Fi in hotels, SIM cards available at airport ($10-20 for data package). Our tours include portable Wi-Fi devices for continuous connectivity.
