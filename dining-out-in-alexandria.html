Chapter 1: Traditional Egyptian Restaurants: Where to Eat?

The city is home to some of the top restaurants in Alexandria, Egypt, where you can savor authentic traditional Egyptian dishes. These top restaurants provide a memorable journey through Egyptian culinary heritage, making Alexandria a food lover's paradise.

🍽️ Top Spots for Traditional Egyptian Dishes

Balbaa Village

A must-visit destination for anyone looking to dive into traditional Egyptian grilled meats and seafood. Renowned for its exceptional quality and flavor, this popular restaurant delights diners with a diverse menu of succulent kebabs, tender lamb chops, and fresh fish grilled to perfection. Its casual, family-friendly atmosphere makes it an ideal spot for gatherings.

Mohamed Ahmed Foul and Falafel

Well-known for its classic Egyptian breakfast dishes, making it a favorite with both locals and tourists. The restaurant has earned a legendary reputation for its crispy falafel and perfectly scrambled eggs, served with warm pita bread and golden fries. Don't miss the traditional foul medames, a flavorful fava bean dish.

More Traditional Favorites

1

Abdel Wahab Village Fine Dining

A distinguished barbecue restaurant offering a fine dining experience that brings a refined twist to traditional Egyptian meat dishes. The menu features mouthwatering grilled specialties, such as succulent kebabs and tender kofta, served alongside delicious stuffed grape leaves and fragrant white rice. The elegant atmosphere and renowned hospitality make it perfect for celebratory gatherings.

2

El-Sayed Mohammed Karim Coastal Seafood

An iconic Alexandria restaurant specializing in traditional Egyptian seafood, drawing in both locals and international visitors with its unique coastal atmosphere. Situated near Alexandria's Corniche, this restaurant offers breathtaking views of the Mediterranean. Diners can indulge in specialties like grilled fish, shrimp, and calamari, all expertly prepared and infused with local herbs and spices.

3

Alban Sewisra Modern Traditional

Celebrated for its innovative approach to traditional Egyptian cuisine while preserving authentic flavors. The restaurant skillfully blends creativity with heritage, resulting in familiar and exciting dishes. One of the menu highlights is the delicious koshary, proudly served with an optional twist that adds a contemporary flair to this beloved favorite.

Chapter 3: International Cuisine in Alexandria

Alexandria's rich history enhances its culinary repertoire with Mediterranean and Middle Eastern flavors. Here are some top spots for international cuisine with an Egyptian twist.

Chez Gaby

A delightful Italian restaurant offering a unique take on Italian cuisine infused with local Egyptian ingredients. Famous for its homemade ice cream and crispy homemade chips.

Sahar El-Laialy

A charming Lebanese restaurant showcasing the rich flavors of Levantine cuisine. The mezze platter is a highlight, offering a delightful assortment of appetizers that encourage sharing.

The Greek Club

A stunning Greek-inspired restaurant located by the historic Citadel of Qaitbay. Combines Mediterranean seafood and meze with breathtaking views of the corniche.

La Gourmandise

An elegant dining destination that masterfully combines French culinary techniques with Egyptian flavors. Known for exquisite desserts, pastries, and a sophisticated atmosphere.

Chapter 4: Hidden Gems: Lesser-Known Restaurants in Alexandria

For those willing to venture off the beaten path, Alexandria offers incredible hidden gems that provide authentic local experiences.

  • El Lol: Tucked away in a corner of Alexandria, this quaint gem delights diners with authentic Egyptian cuisine, particularly fresh seafood. You can pick your own fish and have it cooked to your preference. A true local, no-frills dining experience.
  • Samakmak: Renowned for its exceptional seafood in the harbor district. Patrons rave about the fried grouper, grilled shrimp, and calamari tagine. A no-frills gem with reasonable prices and outstanding quality.
  • Negro: A local favorite with no menu. Diners are treated to a delightful surprise of fresh seafood, including shrimp, crabs, fish, and mussels. The eclectic decor and romantic music create an enchanting environment.
  • Teatro Eskendria: Offers an artsy atmosphere that perfectly complements its innovative Egyptian cuisine. Known for generous slices of chocolate cake and a vibrant, creative setting.
  • Jeeda's: Offers a modern take on traditional Egyptian cuisine in a stylish and intimate setting. Known for high-quality tapas, signature paella, and a thoughtfully curated wine list.

Chapter 5: What to Eat? Must-Try Dishes & Desserts

When pondering what to eat in Alexandria, Egypt, be sure to try iconic dishes that reflect the city's rich culinary heritage.

🍛 Iconic Egyptian Dishes

Koshary

A hearty and inexpensive vegan dish celebrated for its satisfying combination of rice, lentils, pasta, and chickpeas. Topped with a spiced tomato sauce and crispy fried onions. A popular street food and staple lunch option.

Fattah

A traditional and festive dish typically enjoyed during special occasions. Features layers of tender beef alongside rice and crispy fried bread, topped with a flavorful red sauce made from garlic, tomatoes, and spices.

Unique Desserts You Need to Try

Don't miss the unique traditional desserts that define the local sweet scene. For more sweet treats, see our Vegan Desserts Guide.

  • Basbousa: A tasty sweet, semolina cake popular across Egypt and the Middle East. Soaked in a fragrant syrup and often garnished with blanched almonds or shredded coconut.
  • Knafeh (or Kunafa): A luscious dessert made from thin, noodle-like dough called kataifi, traditionally filled with sweet cheese and topped with syrup. Particularly popular during Ramadan.
  • Umm Ali (or Om Ali): A classic Egyptian dessert that embodies comfort and tradition, often enjoyed as a warm bread pudding made with flaky pastry, milk, sugar, and nuts.
  • Qatayef (or Atayef): Delightful stuffed mini pancakes especially popular during Ramadan. Filled with nuts or sweet cream, folded into half-moons, and drizzled with syrup.
  • Roz Bil Laban: Rice pudding valued for its cold, rich, and creamy texture. A perfect choice for cooling off. Try it at local spots like Gelaty Makram or Saber Patisserie.

Frequently Asked Questions

What are the best traditional Egyptian restaurants in Alexandria?
Top spots for traditional Egyptian dishes in Alexandria include Balbaa Village for exceptional grilled meats and seafood, Mohamed Ahmed for legendary breakfast dishes like foul and falafel, and Abdel Wahab Village for fine-dining traditional barbecue. El-Sayed Mohammed Karim is also highly recommended for fresh coastal seafood.
What is the best street food to try in Alexandria?
Must-try street foods include Koshary Ala El Sokhn for the best version of Egypt's national dish, Harissa Ahmed Hassanein for authentic Alexandrian semolina dessert, and Kebda El Fallah for famous liver sandwiches. These spots highlight Alexandria's vibrant street food culture.
Is Alexandria known for seafood?
Yes, Alexandria is renowned for its abundant access to fresh Mediterranean seafood. Popular dishes include grilled and fried fish, tender calamari, and succulent shrimp, often served with rice, fragrant spices, and tahini sauce. Restaurants like Samakmak and El Lol are local favorites.
What traditional desserts should I try in Alexandria?
Don't miss unique traditional desserts like Basbousa (semolina cake soaked in syrup), Knafeh (thin noodle dough filled with sweet cheese), Umm Ali (Egyptian bread pudding), and Qatayef (stuffed mini pancakes). For rice pudding, try Roz Bil Laban at local spots like Gelaty Makram or Saber Patisserie.
How can I experience Alexandria's culinary scene on a tour?
You can experience Alexandria's culinary delights by joining our Alexandria Day Trip from Cairo, which includes expert local guides who can take you to the best local restaurants and hidden gems. It's the perfect way to taste the city's Mediterranean and traditional flavors.
