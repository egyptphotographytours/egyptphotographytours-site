Photography & Equipment Photography

We recommend bringing your preferred DSLR or mirrorless camera with versatile lenses. A wide-angle lens (e.g., 16-35mm) is excellent for expansive landscapes and temple interiors, while a telephoto zoom (e.g., 70-200mm) is perfect for capturing details and compressing scenes. A standard zoom (24-70mm) covers most general photography needs.

Essential accessories include extra batteries, memory cards, a sturdy tripod (especially for low-light temple shots and sunrise/sunset scenes), lens cleaning supplies, and protective gear for sand and dust. A polarizing filter can enhance skies and reduce reflections, while neutral density filters are useful for long exposures of the Nile or desert scenes.

Our guide will advise you on specific site regulations and help facilitate any necessary permissions if possible.

For commercial photography or filming, special permits are required and must be arranged well in advance. We can assist with this process for professional photographers. Note that photography restrictions may apply in certain sensitive areas, and flash photography is typically prohibited inside tombs and museums to preserve the ancient artwork.

Travel & Safety Travel

Egypt is generally safe for tourists, and the government has significantly increased security measures around tourist areas. We prioritize your safety and comfort, using trusted drivers and guides, and staying informed on local conditions. Our private tours offer an added layer of security and personalized attention throughout your Egypt travel experience.

We monitor travel advisories closely and adjust itineraries if needed to ensure your safety. All our transportation partners are vetted for safety standards, and we maintain communication with local authorities to stay updated on any regional concerns. Common-sense precautions (like not flashing expensive equipment in crowded areas) are always recommended, as in any tourist destination worldwide.

The cooler months from October to April offer the most comfortable weather for touring and photography in Egypt, with pleasant temperatures and clear skies. However, these are also peak tourist seasons. May, September, and early October can offer a good balance of fewer crowds and still manageable temperatures for photography.

For specific photography goals: November-February provides the clearest air and best light for landscape shots; March-April offers comfortable temperatures with blooming vegetation; while September-October features dramatic skies and softer light. Summer months (June-August) are extremely hot but offer opportunities for unique atmospheric shots with fewer tourists.

Lightweight, breathable clothing (cotton or linen) is ideal for Egypt's climate. Consider modest attire for visiting religious sites (shoulders and knees covered). Sun protection is essential: hat, sunglasses, high-SPF sunscreen. Comfortable walking shoes are a must for exploring archaeological sites. Don't forget any personal medications, a small backpack for daily excursions, and travel adapters (Egypt uses Type C and F outlets, 220V).

For photography: bring all necessary equipment, extra batteries and memory cards, lens cleaning supplies, a dust-proof bag or case, and a lightweight tripod. For evening outings or upscale dining, smart casual attire is appropriate. A light jacket or sweater is recommended for cooler desert evenings and air-conditioned vehicles.

Most nationalities require a visa for Egypt. Many can obtain a visa on arrival at major airports or apply for an e-Visa online prior to travel. We recommend checking the latest visa requirements for your specific nationality with the Egyptian embassy or consulate in your country well in advance of your trip.

The visa on arrival costs $25 USD (payable in cash) and is typically valid for 30 days. For the e-Visa, apply through the official Egyptian government portal at least 7 days before travel. Your passport should be valid for at least 6 months beyond your planned departure date from Egypt. We can provide supporting documents for your visa application if needed.
