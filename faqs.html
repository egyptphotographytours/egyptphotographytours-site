General Egypt Tour Information General Info

Our Egypt photography tours are uniquely designed for photography enthusiasts and travelers who want to capture stunning images of Egypt's ancient wonders. We focus on photography key spots, less crowded times, unique vantage points, and include professional photography guidance from our dedicated tour guides and photographers. You'll not only see Egypt but capture its essence beautifully with expert support.

Our tours are specifically timed for optimal lighting conditions - golden hour at the pyramids, soft light in temples, and magical sunset views along the Nile. We also provide photography tips specific to Egyptian conditions, helping you master techniques for capturing ancient architecture, desert landscapes, and vibrant local culture.

The absolute best times for pyramids photoshoot at Giza are during golden hour (first hour after sunrise and last hour before sunset). The soft, warm light creates magical shadows and highlights on the ancient structures. Sunrise sessions (5-7 AM depending on season) offer the added benefit of fewer tourists, while sunset sessions provide dramatic skies as backdrop.

For specific photography:

  • Sunrise: Best for front-lit pyramids with morning mist
  • Late afternoon: Ideal for side lighting that reveals texture
  • Blue hour: After sunset for pyramids against twilight sky
  • Night photography: Sound and light show times offer unique opportunities

All our Nile cruise packages include guided photography sessions at riverside temples, sunrise/sunset shots from the deck, and special access to less-visited sites along the riverbanks.

Egypt Photography & Equipment Photography

Yes, there are some photography restrictions at Egyptian historical sites:

  • No Flash: Prohibited inside tombs and museums to preserve artifacts
  • Tripod Restrictions: Often requires special permit or prohibited in certain areas
  • Commercial Photography: Requires advance permits and additional fees
  • Specific Prohibitions: No photography in some tombs in Valley of the Kings
  • Mobile Phones: Usually allowed without additional fees

Our guides are knowledgeable about current restrictions and will advise you at each site. We can help arrange special photography permits for professional shoots when needed.

Egypt uses 220V/50Hz electricity with two types of power outlets:

  • Type C (European): Two round pins
  • Type F (Schuko): Two round pins with grounding clips

For photography equipment charging:

  • Bring universal travel adapters that work with Type C/F
  • Most hotels have multiple outlet types
  • Consider bringing a multi-port USB charger for multiple devices
  • Power banks are essential for day trips to archaeological sites

We recommend testing all charging equipment before your Egypt tour departure.

Egypt Travel Safety & Health Travel

Recommended vaccinations for Egypt travel include:

  • Routine Vaccinations: MMR, diphtheria-tetanus-pertussis, varicella, polio
  • Hepatitis A: Recommended for all travelers
  • Hepatitis B: Recommended for longer stays
  • Typhoid: Recommended for most travelers
  • Rabies: For adventure travelers or animal handlers
  • COVID-19: Follow current Egyptian entry requirements

Consult with a travel medicine specialist 4-6 weeks before your Egypt tour. No yellow fever vaccination is required unless arriving from a yellow fever endemic country.

No, tap water is not safe to drink in Egypt. Follow these water safety guidelines:

  • Bottled Water: Drink only sealed bottled water
  • Brushing Teeth: Use bottled water
  • Ice Cubes: Avoid unless from filtered/bottled water source
  • Raw Vegetables/Fruits: Eat only peeled or thoroughly washed items
  • Hot Beverages: Tea and coffee are generally safe as water is boiled

We provide bottled water during all our Egypt tours. Most hotels have water purification systems, but bottled water is still recommended for drinking.
