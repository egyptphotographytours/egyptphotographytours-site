Escape to the Mediterranean Pearl of Egypt

Trade the desert sands of Cairo for the refreshing sea breeze of the Mediterranean on this unforgettable Alexandria Day Trip. Founded by Alexander the Great in 331 BC, Alexandria was once the intellectual capital of the ancient world, home to the legendary Library of Alexandria and the Pharos Lighthouse—one of the Seven Wonders of the Ancient World.

Today, Alexandria remains Egypt's most cosmopolitan city, blending Greco-Roman ruins, Islamic architecture, and vibrant coastal culture. Our private guided tour takes you on a comprehensive journey through the city's greatest highlights, led by a certified Egyptologist who brings 2,300 years of history to life. The Complete Guide to Alexandria – Egypt's Mediterranean Jewel

While Cairo is the heart of ancient Pharaonic Egypt, Alexandria is the soul of its Greco-Roman and modern Mediterranean identity. For travelers seeking a different pace, stunning coastal views, and a deep dive into the Hellenistic period, an Alexandria day trip is an absolute must.

The Legacy of Alexander the Great & Cleopatra

Alexandria was founded in 331 BC by Alexander the Great and later became the capital of Egypt under the Ptolemaic dynasty. It was here that Cleopatra VII, the last active ruler of the Ptolemaic Kingdom, ruled and famously entertained Julius Caesar and Mark Antony. Though much of the ancient royal quarter lies submerged beneath the modern harbor, the city's spirit of intellectual and cultural brilliance remains palpable.

Qaitbay Citadel: Standing on the Shoulders of Giants

The Citadel of Qaitbay is arguably Alexandria's most iconic landmark. Built in the 15th century by the Mamluk Sultan Qaitbay, this formidable fortress was constructed using the very same stones and on the exact foundations of the Pharos Lighthouse—one of the Seven Wonders of the Ancient World, which was destroyed by earthquakes centuries earlier. Standing on its ramparts, you are literally standing where ancient mariners looked for guidance, with panoramic views of the Mediterranean stretching to the horizon.

The Catacombs of Kom El Shoqafa

Descend into the Catacombs of Kom El Shoqafa, a subterranean necropolis that represents a unique fusion of ancient Egyptian, Greek, and Roman funerary art. Discovered accidentally in 1900 when a cart fell into the access shaft, these catacombs date to the 2nd century AD. The intricate carvings show Roman figures dressed in Egyptian pharaonic regalia, highlighting the fascinating cultural melting pot that was ancient Alexandria.

Bibliotheca Alexandrina: A Modern Miracle

No visit to Alexandria is complete without witnessing the Bibliotheca Alexandrina. In a bold architectural triumph, the modern library was built near the site of the ancient Library of Alexandria, which was once the largest and most significant library of the ancient world. The building's massive sun-disk design, carved with characters from 120 different human scripts, is a stunning tribute to the universal pursuit of knowledge.

The Mediterranean Corniche & Montaza Palace

Alexandria's Corniche is a vibrant coastal promenade that pulses with local life. Cafes, fishermen, and families enjoy the sea breeze, offering a stark contrast to the bustling streets of Cairo. Nearby, the Montaza Palace and its expansive gardens offer a glimpse into the royal history of modern Egypt, providing lush, green landscapes and breathtaking Mediterranean vistas perfect for photography.
