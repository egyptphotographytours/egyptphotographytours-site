How to Capture Professional Photos at Alexandria Landmarks

Follow these professional photography steps to capture stunning images at Alexandrian Mediterranean landmarks.

Step 1: Golden Hour Planning at Qaitbay Citadel

Schedule Qaitbay Citadel photography during golden hour (first 2 hours after sunrise or before sunset) for soft, warm Mediterranean lighting. Position subjects with the ancient fortress as backdrop, using natural side lighting to create depth.

Step 2: Mediterranean Composition Techniques

Use rule of thirds for balanced shots along the Corniche, leading lines from the Citadel walls, and foreground elements like palm trees to create depth with Mediterranean backdrop. Capture the dynamic contrast between ancient and modern Alexandria.

Step 3: Architectural Detail Focus at Bibliotheca

For Bibliotheca Alexandrina architecture, use wide-angle lens for stunning interior shots of the main reading room and telephoto for intricate details of the modern design. Capture the play of natural light through the distinctive roof design.

Step 4: Historical Site Portrait Lighting

Position subjects at Catacombs and Pompey's Pillar with ancient Greco-Roman backdrops, using natural Mediterranean light filtered through openings to create dramatic shadows and highlight texture in the stone carvings.

Step 5: Post-Processing for Mediterranean Colors

Edit RAW files for optimal exposure, enhance Mediterranean blues and golden hour sunset colors while maintaining natural look. Adjust white balance to accurately capture the warm stone tones against the cool Mediterranean waters.

Complete Destination Coverage: Everything You Want to See in Alexandria

Catacombs of Kom El Shoqafa - Ancient Burial Site

Your photography adventure begins at the Catacombs of Kom El Shoqafa, one of the Seven Wonders of the Middle Ages. This multi-level underground burial site showcases unique fusion of Egyptian, Greek and Roman architectural styles. Your professional photographer will guide you through:

  • Main Burial Chamber: Perfect angles for capturing ancient carvings and statues
  • Central Rotunda: Architectural photography of the spiral staircase
  • Banquet Hall: Unique shots of ancient dining area for funeral feasts
  • Caracalla Hall: Photography of niches containing human and animal remains

Pompey's Pillar & Roman Theatre - Greco-Roman Heritage

Experience Alexandria's rich Greco-Roman heritage at Pompey's Pillar, the largest ancient column in Alexandria standing 26.85 meters high, and the restored Roman Theatre with its marble seating and mosaic floors.

Qaitbay Citadel - Mediterranean Fortress

Visit the 15th-century Qaitbay Citadel built on the exact site of the ancient Lighthouse of Alexandria. This fortress offers panoramic Mediterranean views and stunning photography opportunities of the sea meeting ancient stone.

Bibliotheca Alexandrina - Modern Architectural Marvel

The stunning Bibliotheca Alexandrina is a revival of the ancient Library of Alexandria. Its distinctive modern design features a tilted circular roof representing the rising sun, offering incredible interior and exterior photography opportunities.

Montaza Palace Gardens & Corniche - Mediterranean Beauty

Immerse in the beauty of Montaza Palace Gardens with its royal palace buildings, beautiful gardens, and private beaches. The Alexandria Corniche offers scenic coastal photography with Stanley Bridge and Mediterranean views.

What to Wear & Bring for 2026 Mediterranean Tour
