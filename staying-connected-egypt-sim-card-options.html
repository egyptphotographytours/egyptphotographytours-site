FAQs: Egypt SIM Cards & Internet for Tourists

Q1: What's the best SIM card for tourists in Egypt in 2026?

Orange Egypt currently offers the best balance of coverage, speed, and tourist-friendly packages for 2026. Their tourist SIM bundles include generous data allowances and work reliably at major tourist sites including the pyramids, Luxor temples, and Red Sea resorts. For those spending most time in Cairo and Giza, Vodafone Egypt provides excellent urban coverage.

Q2: Can I buy a SIM card at Cairo Airport for my pyramids tour?

Yes, absolutely. Cairo International Airport has dedicated tourist SIM card kiosks in all terminals' arrival halls. You can purchase Vodafone, Orange, Etisalat, or WE SIM cards within 15 minutes of landing. These airport SIMs work immediately at the Giza pyramids, Egyptian Museum, and throughout Cairo. While slightly more expensive than city stores, the convenience is worth it for most tourists.

Q3: Do I need a VPN for internet in Egypt during my trip?

While not mandatory, a VPN is highly recommended for Egypt travel. Some websites and VoIP services may have restrictions. A VPN ensures secure connections on hotel WiFi, access to all your usual services, and protection for online banking. Install and test your VPN (ExpressVPN, NordVPN) before arriving in Egypt as some VPN provider sites may be blocked locally.

Q4: How much data do I need for a 10-day Egypt tour?

For a typical 10-day Egypt tour visiting pyramids, temples, and cities, we recommend:

  • Light user (maps, WhatsApp, email): 10-15GB
  • Moderate user (social media, photo uploads, some streaming): 20-30GB
  • Heavy user (video calls, streaming, constant uploads): 40-50GB

Most tourist SIM packages offer 10-30GB for 7-14 day validity at affordable prices.

Q5: Will my SIM card work during Nile cruise and desert tours?

Most major networks provide coverage along the Nile between Luxor and Aswan, though speeds may vary. During desert tours to places like White Desert or Sinai, coverage can be spotty. For Nile cruises, Orange and WE generally have the most reliable river coverage. Always download offline maps before desert excursions.
