Egypt's Climate Regions: What to Expect Where

Egypt's weather varies significantly by region, affecting both comfort and photography conditions. Here's what you need to know for planning your photography tour:

Cairo & Nile Delta Region

Cairo experiences mild winters with daytime highs around 20¬∞C (68¬∞F) and hot summers reaching 34¬∞C (93¬∞F). The city's urban density can make temperatures feel several degrees warmer. March and April can be windy, sometimes causing sandstorms that affect photography conditions. Pollution combined with high temperatures can make summer afternoons particularly challenging for outdoor photography.

Alexandria & Mediterranean Coast

Alexandria enjoys more temperate conditions with sea breezes moderating temperatures year-round. Average highs range from 17¬∞C (63¬∞F) in winter to 29¬∞C (84¬∞F) in summer. This region receives most of Egypt's rainfall (between 20mm and 200mm annually), primarily in winter months. The khamaseen winds from the Sahara can occasionally make conditions uncomfortable, particularly if blowing strongly.

Recommended Photography Tour:

Alexandria Photography Tour - Ideal between March and November for comfortable coastal photography with Mediterranean breezes.

Southern Egypt: Luxor & Aswan

These areas experience Egypt's most extreme heat, with Aswan averaging 41¬∞C (106¬∞F) in summer and Luxor reaching similar highs. These cities have some of the hottest temperatures in Egypt with extremely low humidity in winter (not rising above 42%). Rainfall is almost nonexistent‚ÄîAswan receives less than 1mm annually, with some years having no rainfall at all. Winter months provide brief relief with warmer temperatures compared to northern regions.

Red Sea Coast: Hurghada & Sharm El Sheikh

The Red Sea resorts enjoy sunshine year-round with very low rainfall occurring almost exclusively in winter. Ideal visiting times are early autumn and late spring when temperatures are warm but tourist crowds are smaller. Sea temperatures remain consistently warm, averaging around 24¬∞C (75¬∞F), making year-round swimming and underwater photography possible.

Special Weather Considerations for Photography Tours

Sandstorms (Khamsin)

Occurring between March and June, these hot, dry winds from the Sahara can temporarily raise temperatures above 49¬∞C (120¬∞F) and reduce visibility to near zero. While disruptive, they're usually brief (1-3 days). Our tours build in flexibility to reschedule outdoor sessions when khamsin winds are forecasted.

Nile River Conditions

Nile cruising is most pleasant October-April when temperatures are cooler and rainfall minimal. Summer cruises are still enjoyable with air-conditioned comforts and beautiful early morning photography opportunities on deck.

Desert Photography Considerations

Desert excursions (White Desert, Siwa Oasis) are best in winter months when the risk of heat exhaustion is lower. Nighttime temperatures can drop significantly, especially in winter when desert regions can experience freezing conditions. We provide appropriate guidance and equipment for desert photography in all seasons.

Packing Guide for Egypt's Climate

Proper preparation ensures comfort and better photography results:

  • October-April: Light layers for daytime, warmer jacket for evenings. Sun protection essential.
  • May-September: Lightweight, breathable clothing. Wide-brimmed hat, high SPF sunscreen. Swimwear for Red Sea tours.
  • Photography Equipment: Lens cleaning kits for dust/sand. UV filters for bright conditions. Extra batteries (heat affects performance).
  • Footwear: Comfortable walking shoes for archaeological sites, sand-friendly shoes for desert excursions.
  • Always Include: Scarf for potential sandstorms, reusable water bottle, portable power bank.
