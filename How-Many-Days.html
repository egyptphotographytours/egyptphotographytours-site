How Many Days Do You Need in Egypt?

From a focused 7-day highlights trip to a 17-day deep dive — find the itinerary length that matches your travel style, budget and curiosity.

Home/ Blog/ How Many Days in Egypt
Updated regularly 20 min read Cairo, Luxor & Aswan, Egypt By Hossam, Egyptologist & Photographer

One of the most common questions we hear when planning a trip is simple: how many days do you actually need in Egypt? With so much to see — ancient temples, the bustling markets of Islamic Cairo, desert oases, and the timeless Nile — getting the length right matters more than almost any other planning decision you'll make.

Egypt is a country of extraordinary contrasts. In a focused week, you can stand before the Pyramids of Giza and sail past temples that have stood for over three thousand years. With more time, your trip can expand to Luxor, Aswan, Abu Simbel, the desert oases, and even a few days unwinding on the Red Sea. This guide breaks down exactly what each trip length includes, so you can choose with confidence — or let our team at Egypt Photography Tours build it for you.

Short on Time: 7–8 Days in Egypt

Even a week is enough to capture Egypt's essence if the itinerary is planned well. A 7-8 day trip blends Cairo's icons with a focused taste of the Nile, without feeling like a sprint.

7-Day Itinerary

Cairo & Giza Pyramids Grand Egyptian Museum Luxor temples & Valley of the Kings

Built for travelers who want the headline experiences without compromise. Days begin in Cairo with private-feeling early access to the Great Pyramid and the Sphinx, time at the Pyramids of Giza, and the Grand Egyptian Museum. A flight to Luxor opens up Karnak Temple, Luxor Temple, and the Valley of the Kings with an expert Egyptologist guide. Most 7-day itineraries close with a return to Cairo for the Citadel of Salah El-Din and an evening at Khan El Khalili Bazaar.

8-Day Itinerary

Cairo highlights Abu Simbel Short Nile cruise

An extra day allows a short Nile cruise to be added alongside a flight to Abu Simbel, where the colossal rock-cut temples of Ramses II never fail to impress. In Cairo, time is also set aside for Saqqara and the National Museum of Egyptian Civilization. This length balances efficiency with genuine depth — see our complete 48 Hours in Cairo guide for how those opening days typically unfold.

The Balanced Choice: 10–12 Days in Egypt

For most first-time visitors, 10-12 days is the sweet spot — enough time to see everything on the essential list at a comfortable pace, with room left over for a Red Sea extension or a deeper look at Cairo.

10-Day Itinerary

Saqqara & Dahshur 4-5 night Nile cruise Abu Simbel & Philae

This itinerary opens in Cairo with Saqqara and Dahshur's pyramids alongside the Grand Egyptian Museum, the Citadel, and Khan El Khalili. A flight to Luxor leads into an extended Nile cruise covering the Valley of the Kings, the Temple of Hatshepsut, Karnak and Luxor Temple, continuing south through Edfu and Kom Ombo to Aswan. Highlights there include Abu Simbel, a Nubian village visit, the Unfinished Obelisk, and the Temple of Isis at Philae, before returning to Cairo for an exclusive, less-crowded visit to the Giza Plateau.

12-Day Itinerary

Everything in the 10-day trip Red Sea relaxation Optional Alexandria add-on

Two additional days open up real downtime. After the temples and the Nile cruise, the itinerary shifts to Hurghada for snorkeling, diving, or simply relaxing by turquoise water — see our guide to the best all-inclusive Red Sea resorts for options. Returning to Cairo, there's time for the Egyptian Museum, Historic Cairo, and an optional day trip to Alexandria before flying home.

In-Depth Exploration: 14–17 Days in Egypt

For travelers who want more than the headline monuments — deeper cultural immersion, hidden Nile sites, and the desert oases — a 14 to 17 day trip is genuinely transformative.

14-Day Itinerary

Fayoum Oasis & Wadi El Hitan 7-night Nile cruise Relaxed, unhurried pace

Ideal for travelers who prefer a gentler pace, this itinerary pairs Saqqara and Dahshur with an excursion to the Fayoum Oasis and Wadi El Hitan (Whale Valley), a UNESCO World Heritage Site where ancient whale fossils sit among desert lakes. The centerpiece is a full 7-night Nile cruise between Luxor and Aswan, allowing the Valley of the Kings, Kom Ombo, Edfu, Abu Simbel and Philae to be explored without ever feeling rushed.

17-Day Signature Itinerary

Siwa Oasis Alexandria Lesser-visited Nile sites

Our most complete itinerary covers nearly every major site along the Nile while also venturing to the remote Siwa Oasis — palm groves, salt lakes, the Oracle Temple, and the desert hot springs of Bir Wahed. The route also takes in Alexandria's Bibliotheca and catacombs, plus a long Nile journey passing rarely visited sites like Tuna el-Gebel, Abydos, and Dendera, alongside the classic stops in Luxor and Aswan. This length suits dedicated history and culture enthusiasts who want the complete picture.

Choosing the Right Length for You

The right answer isn't only about how many days you have available — it's about what you want most from the trip. Shorter 7-8 day itineraries suit travelers who want Cairo, the Pyramids, and a meaningful taste of the Nile without feeling rushed. The 10-12 day range is where most first-time visitors land, balancing sightseeing with a little relaxation. Longer 14-17 day journeys reward travelers who want to go beyond the headline sites into Egypt's oases, lesser-visited temples, and a genuinely unhurried pace.

Whichever length you choose, timing matters too — see our complete Egypt weather guide for the best months to travel, and our budget planning guide if cost is a key factor in your decision.

Frequently Asked Questions

What is the minimum number of days needed to see Egypt's highlights?
Seven days is a realistic minimum: the Pyramids of Giza, the Egyptian Museum, and a short Nile cruise or flight to Luxor for the Valley of the Kings and Karnak Temple. It's a fuller pace, but genuinely achievable.
Is a week enough to visit Egypt?
A week is enough for a well-planned highlights trip covering Cairo and either Luxor or a short Nile cruise. It won't allow for the oases, Abu Simbel, or a Red Sea extension, but it captures the essential pyramids-to-temples experience.
How many days should I spend in Cairo alone?
Two to three days is comfortable for the Pyramids of Giza, the Grand Egyptian Museum, Islamic Cairo, and Khan El Khalili, with a little buffer for Saqqara or Dahshur. Our 48 Hours in Cairo guide works well as a template.
What is the ideal length for a Nile cruise?
Three to four nights covers the essential stops between Luxor and Aswan, including Karnak, the Valley of the Kings, Edfu and Kom Ombo. Travelers with more time often prefer a seven-night cruise for a noticeably more relaxed pace.
Can I see the Pyramids and Luxor in one trip without rushing?
Yes, with at least 8-10 days total: roughly 2-3 days in Cairo plus a 3-4 night Nile cruise or Luxor/Aswan stay, with a day or two of buffer for travel between cities.
How many days do families need in Egypt?
Most families do well with 8-12 days, allowing a relaxed pace through Cairo and a Nile cruise without feeling rushed, plus the option to add Red Sea resort time for downtime between sightseeing days.
Is two weeks too long for a first visit to Egypt?
Not at all. Two weeks comfortably covers Cairo, an extended Nile cruise, Abu Simbel, and either a desert oasis or a Red Sea extension, without ever feeling rushed between sites.
Should I add a Red Sea extension to my Egypt itinerary?
If your trip is 10 days or longer, a 2-4 night Red Sea extension in Hurghada or Sharm El Sheikh is a popular way to balance intensive sightseeing with relaxation before flying home.
What's included in a typical 10-day Egypt itinerary?
Typically 2-3 days in Cairo (Pyramids, Saqqara, the Egyptian Museum), a flight to Luxor, a 4-5 night Nile cruise to Aswan with stops at Edfu and Kom Ombo, and a visit to Abu Simbel, before returning to Cairo.

Final Thoughts: There's No Single Right Answer

The perfect Egypt itinerary length is the one that matches your curiosity, your pace, and the experiences you most want to have. A focused week delivers the Pyramids and the Nile without compromise. Ten to twelve days is where most travelers find their balance. And for those who want to go further — into the oases, the quieter temples, and the rhythm of a longer Nile journey — two to three weeks rewards every extra day.
