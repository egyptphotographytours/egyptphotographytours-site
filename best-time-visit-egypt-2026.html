Part 1: Egypt's Climate at a Glance

Egypt is mostly desert—hot, dry, and bathed in sunshine. But "hot" is relative. The country spans from the Mediterranean coast (Alexandria) to deep Saharan outposts (Abu Simbel). Understanding regional variation is key. For a deeper dive into specific regions, read our Egypt Destinations Guide.

Northern Egypt
Cairo, Alexandria, Delta. Mild winters (10-20°C), hot summers (30-35°C). Brief winter rain possible. Best October–April.
Central & Southern
Luxor, Aswan. Arid desert. Winters warm (20-25°C), summers extreme (40-45°C). Virtually no rain. Huge diurnal swings.
Red Sea Coast
Hurghada, Sharm. Semi-desert, moderated by sea. Winters pleasant, summers hot but breezy. Perfect for diving year-round.
Key Weather Patterns
Sandstorm Season (Khamsin): Mar-May. Rainfall: Negligible (except North in winter). Sunshine: Year-round (8-9 hrs daily).

Part 3: Month-by-Month Deep Dive

We now dissect each month with surgical precision: average temperatures, crowd indices, cultural events, and photographic opportunities.

🇪🇬 January – The Cool, Clear Peak

Weather: Cairo: 9-19°C; Luxor: 8-23°C. Days are sunny and pleasant, nights are genuinely cold—bring a jacket (see our Packing List).

Crowds: Extremely high. Christmas holidays bring massive influxes. The Pyramids and Luxor temples are packed.

Photography: Crisp air, long shadows, golden light. Early morning at the Pyramids (7 AM) is essential. Our Private Pyramids Photography Tour gets you exclusive access during the best light.

🇪🇬 February – Still Prime, Slightly Quieter

Weather: Cairo: 11-21°C; Luxor: 9-25°C. Warming slowly, still ideal.

Events: Abu Simbel Sun Festival (Feb 22) – sunlight illuminates the inner sanctuary of Ramses II's temple. Incredible spectacle.

Tour Tip: Combine Cairo with Abu Simbel on a custom 7-Day Discovery Tour timed for the festival.

🇪🇬 March – Spring Arrives, Khamsin Begins

Weather: Cairo: 13-24°C; Luxor: 14-29°C. Warming fast. Khamsin winds can bring dust storms late month.

Photography: Soft spring light, potential for dramatic dust-filtered sunsets. Ideal for Alexandria day trips where sea breezes moderate temperatures.

🇪🇬 April – Warm, Windy & Festive

Weather: Cairo: 17-28°C; Luxor: 20-34°C. Can feel hot in Luxor.

Events: Sham el-Nessim (spring festival) – Egyptians flock to parks and the Nile. A unique cultural experience. Learn more in our Festivals Calendar.

Family Tip: Great month for a Family Tour – kids will enjoy the festive atmosphere.

🇪🇬 May – Shoulder to Summer Transition

Weather: Cairo: 20-33°C; Luxor: 24-38°C. Hot in the south, pleasant mornings in Cairo.

Activities: Last chance for comfortable Nile cruising before summer. Consider our 7-Day Egypt Discovery with early start times.

🇪🇬 June – Summer Scorcher, Low Season Bargains

Weather: Cairo: 23-35°C; Luxor: 27-41°C. Intense sun. Start days at 6 AM.

Strategy: Focus on Red Sea (Hurghada) where breezes cool things down, or combine with a Nile cruise (indoor cabins). Our Honeymoon Tours offer romantic private transfers with AC.

🇪🇬 July – Hottest Month, Vibrant Nights

Weather: Cairo: 25-36°C; Luxor: 28-42°C. Record highs possible.

Photography: Harsh light midday, but sunrise and sunset are spectacular. Use indoor sites (Egyptian Museum) during peak heat. Our 2-Day Cairo & Giza tour is designed with air-conditioned vehicles and optimal timing.

🇪🇬 August – Peak Heat, Peak Deals

Weather: Similar to July. Wafaa El-Nil (Nile flood festival) mid-month.

Prices: Absolute lowest. Perfect for introverts wanting solitude at the Valley of the Kings.

🇪🇬 September – Autumn Shoulder, Heat Lingers

Weather: Cairo: 23-34°C; Luxor: 25-39°C. Still hot in early Sept, cools by month's end.

Events: Abu Simbel Sun Festival (Sept 22) – the other illumination day.

🇪🇬 October – The Golden Month

Weather: Cairo: 20-30°C; Luxor: 22-35°C. Idyllic—warm days, cool evenings.

Photography: The light is perfect—low angle, golden hours long. Book our Private Pyramids Tour for uninterrupted shoots.

🇪🇬 November – Prime Time Begins

Weather: Cairo: 16-26°C; Luxor: 18-31°C. Beautiful, crisp.

Cruising: Ideal for Nile cruises. The weather on deck is sublime.

🇪🇬 December – Christmas Cheer & Crowds

Weather: Cairo: 11-21°C; Luxor: 10-25°C. Cool, some rain possible north.

Experience: Festive atmosphere. For a stress-free holiday, our Family Tours handle all logistics.

Part 4: Timing by Activity

📸 For Photographers: The Light Chaser's Calendar

  • Best overall: October, November, February, March – soft light, long shadows, clear skies.
  • Pyramids at sunrise: Year-round possible, but winter months have later sunrises making early starts less painful.
  • Desert camps: September–November and March–May – comfortable nights for sleeping under stars.

Our Private Pyramids Photography Tour is designed for optimal light year-round. Ensure you have the right gear with our Photography Gear Guide.

🚢 For Nile Cruises

Prime season: October to April. Temperatures allow lounging on deck. Summer cruises are heavily discounted but too hot for daytime deck enjoyment—though the cabins are air-conditioned and the temples are empty.

👨‍👩‍👧‍👦 For Families

Sweet spots: March-April and September-October. Warm but not scorching, fewer crowds than winter. Our Family Photography Tours include kid-focused guides and flexible pacing.

Part 7: Special 2026 Considerations

Ramadan 2026 (approx. Feb 18 – Mar 19)

Ramadan shifts annually; in 2026 it falls in late winter/early spring. During Ramadan, many restaurants are closed until sunset, but evenings are festive. Tourist sites operate on reduced hours but are less crowded. It's a unique cultural experience—read our Ramadan in Egypt Guide for detailed etiquette.

Frequently Asked Questions

What is the best time to visit Egypt for good weather?
October to April offers the most comfortable temperatures for sightseeing, with daytime highs between 20-28°C (68-82°F). This is peak tourist season, perfect for exploring temples and Nile cruises.
When is the cheapest time to visit Egypt?
June through August is the low season, with hotel rates dropping 40-60% and significantly cheaper flights. The intense heat keeps crowds away, but Red Sea resorts remain pleasant for diving.
What are the crowd levels like at the Pyramids in winter?
December to February sees the highest crowds. To avoid them, visit early morning (7:00 AM) or book a private tour like our Private Pyramids Photography Tour for exclusive access.
Is summer too hot for a Nile cruise?
Summer temperatures in Luxor and Aswan can exceed 40°C (104°F). While cabins are air-conditioned, daytime deck time is limited. However, you'll enjoy significant discounts. Winter (Nov-Feb) is prime for cruises.
When is the best time for photography in Egypt?
Spring (March-April) and Autumn (September-November) offer the best light—golden hours with clear skies. The angle of the sun during these months creates dramatic shadows on temple reliefs and pyramid landscapes.
