Understanding Egyptian Culture & Travel Etiquette

Egypt is a country with rich traditions and warm hospitality. Understanding local customs enhances your travel experience and fosters meaningful connections:

  • Greetings: Egyptians are generally warm and welcoming. A handshake is common, though some conservative women may prefer not to shake hands with men. The traditional greeting "As-salamu alaykum" (Peace be upon you) is appreciated.
  • Dress Code: While urban areas are relatively liberal, modest dress is appreciated, especially at religious sites. Shoulders and knees should be covered when visiting mosques and churches.
  • Photography Etiquette: Always ask permission before photographing people, especially women and children. At some sites, photography may require additional permits or fees.
  • Bargaining: Expected in markets (souks) but not in established shops. Approach with good humor and respect - it's a social interaction, not a confrontation.
  • Tipping (Baksheesh): Tipping is customary for services. Small amounts (5-20 Egyptian pounds) are appropriate for hotel staff, drivers, and guides.
  • Religious Sensitivity: Friday is the Muslim day of prayer, and some businesses may close for a few hours. During Ramadan, be discreet about eating and drinking in public during daylight hours.

Best Time to Visit Egypt: Seasonal Considerations

Egypt's climate varies significantly by season and region. Choosing the right time for your visit enhances comfort and experience:

  • October-April (Peak Season): Ideal weather with pleasant temperatures (20-25¬∞C/68-77¬∞F). Clear skies perfect for photography. This is the busiest tourist season requiring advance booking.
  • May-September (Summer Season): Hotter temperatures (30-40¬∞C/86-104¬∞F) but fewer crowds and lower prices. Coastal areas remain comfortable. Early morning and evening activities recommended.
  • December-January (Winter Peak): Coolest months with occasional rain in northern areas. Peak tourist season around holidays requires booking well in advance.
  • Shoulder Seasons (March-April & October-November): Pleasant weather with moderate crowds. Ideal for photography with good light conditions.
  • Ramadan: The Islamic holy month offers unique cultural experiences. Dates change annually based on the lunar calendar.

Egypt Tour Planning: Essential Information

Proper planning ensures a smooth and enjoyable Egypt experience:

  • Visa Requirements: Most nationalities can obtain a visa on arrival at Egyptian airports (USD $25) or apply for an e-visa online before travel. Passports must be valid for at least six months.
  • Health & Safety: Travel insurance is highly recommended. Basic vaccinations should be up to date. Drink bottled water and practice standard food safety precautions.
  • Currency: Egyptian Pound (EGP). Credit cards accepted at major hotels and restaurants, but cash is essential for markets and smaller establishments.
  • Transportation: Domestic flights connect major cities. Trains offer scenic routes. Private drivers and Nile cruises provide comfortable intercity travel.
  • Communication: Arabic is the official language. English is widely spoken in tourist areas. Learning basic Arabic phrases enhances interactions.
  • Electrical: 220V, 50Hz. Type C and F plugs are standard. Adapters are readily available.
