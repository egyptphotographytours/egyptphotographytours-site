2. Cultural Guide for Solo Women in Egypt

Understanding and respecting Egyptian culture is essential for a smooth and enjoyable journey. Egypt is a predominantly Muslim country with conservative values in many areas, though tourist destinations tend to be more liberal.

Dress Code Guide for Female Travelers

Dressing appropriately shows respect for local culture and significantly reduces unwanted attention. Here's our comprehensive packing guide:

Urban Areas (Cairo, Alexandria)

Knees and shoulders covered. Loose-fitting clothes in breathable fabrics. Light scarves handy for mosques.

Tourist Sites (Pyramids, Temples)

Comfortable walking clothes with sun protection. Hats, sunglasses essential. Modest but practical.

Resort Areas (Red Sea)

More liberal. Swimwear acceptable at pools/beaches. Cover up when leaving resort premises.

Religious Sites

Headscarves required for women in mosques. Long sleeves, ankle-length skirts or trousers.

Cultural Do's and Don'ts

Cultural Do's:

  • Do greet with "As-salamu alaykum" (Peace be upon you) - it's appreciated
  • Do accept tea invitations from shopkeepers - it's a sign of hospitality
  • Do remove shoes before entering mosques and Egyptian homes
  • Do use your right hand for eating and handing items
  • Do bargain respectfully in markets - it's expected and part of the culture

Cultural Don'ts:

  • Don't show public displays of affection - holding hands is acceptable for married couples
  • Don't photograph people without permission - always ask first
  • Don't drink alcohol in public places outside tourist areas
  • Don't discuss sensitive political topics unless initiated by locals
  • Don't visit religious sites during prayer times unless you're there to pray

9. Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)

Is Egypt safe for solo women travelers in 2026?

Yes, Egypt is generally safe for solo women travelers when proper precautions are taken. With our women-only tours featuring female guides, secure accommodations, and private transportation, you can explore Egypt with complete confidence. Tourist areas have excellent security, and millions of women travel to Egypt safely each year.

What should solo women wear in Egypt?

Modest clothing covering shoulders and knees is recommended. Loose-fitting, breathable fabrics are ideal for the climate. In tourist areas like the pyramids, comfortable walking clothes are fine. For mosques, women need headscarves and clothing covering arms and legs. At Red Sea resorts, normal swimwear is acceptable at hotel pools and beaches.

Are there women-only Egypt tours available?

Yes, we specialize in women-only Egypt tours with female Egyptologist guides. Our tours are designed specifically for solo female travelers, with women-only groups, female guides and drivers, and accommodations that prioritize women's safety and comfort.
