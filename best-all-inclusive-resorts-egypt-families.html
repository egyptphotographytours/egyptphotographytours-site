Top 10 All‑Inclusive Family Resorts in Egypt (2026)

1. Makadi Palace & Spa – Hurghada
Makadi Bay, Hurghada
Kids: Mini Club (4‑12), Splash Park
5 pools + private beach
7 restaurants, 24h room service

Makadi Palace is a perennial favorite for families. The sprawling grounds feel like a lush paradise, with a dedicated kids' pool featuring slides and a pirate ship. The Mini Club runs daily activities (face painting, treasure hunts) and there's even a babysitting service (nominal fee) so parents can enjoy a romantic dinner at the seafood restaurant. Family suites are enormous, many with kitchenettes. Insider tip: Book a “family bungalow” right next to the kids' pool.

Combine with: A private 2‑day Cairo & Pyramids tour – we'll pick you up from Makadi Bay, fly you to Cairo, and have you back in time for sunset.

2. Steigenberger Aqua Magic – Hurghada
Safaga Road, Hurghada
Massive waterpark (18 slides)
Baby club (0‑3), kids' club
10 restaurants + food trucks

If your kids love waterslides, this is the Holy Grail. Steigenberger Aqua Magic is built around an enormous waterpark with attractions for all ages – from a lazy river to adrenaline‑pumping slides. The all‑inclusive covers soft drinks, ice cream, and even some premium brands. The kids' club (4‑12) offers science experiments, cooking classes, and mini‑disco every night. Teens have a dedicated hangout with gaming consoles. Pro tip: Book a room in the “family wing” for direct pool access.

Add the 7‑day Egypt Discovery tour – after your resort stay, we'll whisk you to Luxor and Aswan for a Nile cruise.

3. Baron Palace Resort – Sahl Hasheesh
Sahl Hasheesh, Hurghada
5‑star luxury, butler service
Kids' club + splash park
Adults‑only spa area

For families seeking a touch of opulence, Baron Palace delivers. The architecture is palatial, rooms are grand, and service is impeccable. The kids' club is open 10am‑10pm, and the shallow lagoon beach is perfect for little ones. Multiple pools include a heated one in winter. Parents can sneak away to the serene spa while teens enjoy the sports court. All‑inclusive includes a la carte dining at Italian, Asian, and Egyptian restaurants.

Photography opportunity: Book a Private Pyramids photography session – we arrange a sunrise shoot with a professional photographer who knows the best angles for family portraits.

4. Rixos Sharm El Sheikh – Naama Bay
Naama Bay, Sharm El Sheikh
Kids' camp, mini‑disco
Private beach, snorkeling
Sports academy for teens

Rixos is synonymous with luxury and entertainment. The “Rixy Kids Club” is one of the best in the region, with a full schedule of activities: pottery, magic shows, and even a mini‑disco that gets everyone dancing. The resort's private beach offers gentle entry to the Red Sea, ideal for snorkeling. Family rooms are spacious and many interconnect. The all‑inclusive includes 24‑hour dining and imported drinks.

Extend your adventure with a day trip to Alexandria from Sharm (we arrange flights) to see the catacombs and library.

5. Jaz Aquaviva – Hurghada
Makadi Bay, Hurghada
Aqua park with 50+ slides
Kids' world (4‑12), baby pool
12 restaurants, 7 bars

Jaz Aquaviva is a waterpark resort on steroids. With over 50 slides and attractions, it's a paradise for kids and kids‑at‑heart. The all‑inclusive is incredibly comprehensive: all meals, snacks, ice cream, and soft drinks. The family rooms are modern and some have bunk beds. Evening entertainment includes live shows and a mini‑disco. Note: The waterpark is free for guests, but you must wear wristbands.

Browse all our tours to add a cultural day trip – perhaps a visit to Luxor's temples?

6. Sunrise Royal Makadi – Makadi Bay
Makadi Bay, Hurghada
Superb house reef snorkeling
Sports hub, teen club
Organic kids' menu

This resort is perfect for active families. The house reef is accessible right from the beach, offering world‑class snorkeling. Kids can join the “Sunrise Explorers” program with guided reef walks and marine biology lessons. The all‑inclusive is top‑notch, with several a la carte restaurants and a buffet that caters to even the pickiest eaters. There's also a large sports center with tennis, football, and archery.

What to Look for in a Family All‑Inclusive Resort

  • Kids' Club Quality: Look for supervised, age‑appropriate activities with extended hours. Ask if they have a baby club (under 3).
  • Water Features: Pools with shallow areas, splash pads, and slides are a must.
  • Dining Flexibility: Buffets with kid‑friendly options, high chairs, and early dinner seatings.
  • Accommodation: Family suites, connecting rooms, or villas with kitchenettes.
  • Babysitting: Some resorts offer certified babysitters (usually for a fee) – perfect for a date night.

Q: What's the best time to visit for families?
