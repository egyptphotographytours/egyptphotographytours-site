Why Egypt for a Family All-Inclusive Vacation?

Egypt's Red Sea coast offers year-round sunshine, calm and clear turquoise water, and some of the most generous all-inclusive packages anywhere. Resorts here are genuinely built with families in mind: large pools with shallow sections, dedicated kids' buffets, professional entertainment teams, and — at many properties — full on-site waterparks.

Beyond the beach, Egypt offers a "wow" factor few other beach destinations can match. The Pyramids of Giza, the Sphinx, and the Grand Egyptian Museum are all reachable on a short flight from the resort hubs of Hurghada and Sharm El Sheikh, letting you combine relaxed beach time with a once-in-a-lifetime cultural experience.

What to Look for in a Family All-Inclusive Resort

  • Kids' club quality: Supervised, age-appropriate activities with reasonably long hours, plus a baby club if you have a child under 3.
  • Water features: Shallow pool sections, splash pads, and a few slides go a long way for younger children.
  • Dining flexibility: Buffets with kid-friendly options, high chairs, and early dinner seatings.
  • Accommodation: Family suites, connecting rooms, or villas with kitchenettes.
  • Babysitting: Some resorts offer paid, certified babysitting — useful for an occasional parents' night out.

Top All-Inclusive Family Resorts in Egypt

Here are the family resorts that consistently stand out across Egypt's Red Sea coast:

1

Makadi Palace & Spa

Makadi Bay, Hurghada
Mini Club (4–12) Shallow kids' pool Family suites with kitchenette options

A perennial favorite for families, with lush grounds and a dedicated kids' pool. The mini club runs daily supervised activities, and babysitting can typically be arranged for a parents' evening out.

Combine with: A private 2-day Cairo & Pyramids family tour, with pickup from Makadi Bay.

2

Steigenberger Aqua Magic

Hurghada
On-site waterpark Baby club + kids' club Multiple dining venues

Built around an extensive waterpark with slides for a range of ages, this resort is a natural pick for families with slide-obsessed kids. The kids' club runs cooking classes and themed activity days, with a separate hangout space for teens.

3

Baron Palace Resort

Sahl Hasheesh, Hurghada
5-star, palatial design Kids' club + splash park Adults-only spa zone

For families wanting a touch of luxury, Baron Palace delivers grand architecture and a shallow lagoon beach that's gentle for young swimmers. Multiple a la carte restaurants give parents dining variety beyond the main buffet.

Photography opportunity: Pair your stay with a private Pyramids photography session for a professional family portrait against an unforgettable backdrop.

4

Rixos Sharm El Sheikh

Naama Bay, Sharm El Sheikh
Supervised kids' camp Private beach, gentle entry Sports academy for teens

Rixos leans into entertainment, with a packed daily schedule for kids and a private beach with calm, shallow entry that's well suited to first-time snorkelers. Spacious, often interconnecting, family rooms round out the appeal.

Extend the trip with a day trip to Alexandria for older kids interested in history.

5

Jaz Aquaviva

Makadi Bay, Hurghada
Multi-slide waterpark Kids' world + baby pool Extensive dining options

Another waterpark-forward resort, with a comprehensive all-inclusive plan that typically extends to snacks and ice cream throughout the day. Modern family rooms, some with bunk beds, make this a practical pick for larger families.

6

Sunrise Royal Makadi

Makadi Bay, Hurghada
House-reef snorkeling Large sports center Kid-friendly dining

Ideal for active families, with snorkeling accessible straight from the beach and a sports center offering tennis, football and more. Younger kids can join guided reef-walk and marine-life activities through the kids' programming.

7

Three Corners Rihana Resort

Marsa Alam
Renowned house reef Quieter, eco-conscious setting Family-friendly pacing

Marsa Alam moves at a slower pace than Hurghada or Sharm, and Three Corners Rihana is a great match for families who want excellent shore snorkeling without the bustle of a larger resort town.

8

Pickalbatros Resorts

Hurghada & Sharm El Sheikh
Strong value Dedicated aqua park properties Full entertainment programs

A resort group with several properties across both coasts, popular for budget-conscious families who still want a dedicated waterpark and a full daily activity schedule.

Best Time to Visit for a Family Resort Trip

Spring (March–May) and autumn (September–November) offer the most comfortable beach weather for families, with warm but manageable temperatures. Winter (December–February) is mild and very pleasant for sightseeing, while summer is hot but resorts are well air-conditioned and pools see plenty of use. For a region-by-region breakdown across the whole country, see our complete Egypt weather guide.

What to Pack for a Red Sea Family Resort Trip

  • Reef-safe, high-SPF sunscreen for the whole family.
  • Swim shoes for coral-lined beach entries.
  • Light layers for air-conditioned rooms and restaurants.
  • Basic snorkeling gear if your kids enjoy the water — many resorts rent it too.
  • Modest cover-ups for any side trip to a mosque or cultural site, and don't forget your Egypt visa is sorted before you fly.
  • A local SIM or eSIM — see our guide to staying connected in Egypt for easy navigation and video calls home.

Frequently Asked Questions

Which all-inclusive resort in Egypt is best for toddlers?
Resorts with a dedicated baby or mini club, shallow pools, and family suites with kitchenettes work best. Makadi Palace in Hurghada is a popular pick thanks to its shallow kids' pool and supervised mini club.
Do all-inclusive resorts in Egypt have kids' clubs?
Yes — most top family resorts feature supervised kids' clubs, typically ages 4–12, with separate programming for teens at larger properties, and many also offer baby clubs for children under 3.
Can we combine a resort stay with tours to the Pyramids?
Yes. Private day trips from Hurghada or Sharm El Sheikh to Cairo are popular and straightforward — see our 48 Hours in Cairo guide for a complete itinerary framework.
What is the best time of year for a family resort vacation in Egypt?
March–May and September–November typically offer the most comfortable beach weather. Winter is mild and pleasant for sightseeing, while summer is hot but resorts stay cool with air conditioning and busy pools.
Are there all-inclusive resorts with waterparks in Egypt?
Yes — several Hurghada resorts are built around extensive on-site waterparks with slides for a range of ages, in addition to standard pools and splash zones for younger kids.
Do you offer photography services at the resorts?
We don't operate inside individual resorts, but we can arrange a professional family photography session during a day excursion, such as a private Pyramids visit, or coordinate a session near your resort.
How far is Hurghada or Sharm El Sheikh from Cairo and the Pyramids?
Both are roughly a one-hour flight from Cairo, making a guided day trip to the Pyramids a realistic and popular add-on to a Red Sea resort stay.
Is Egypt safe for family travel?
Egypt's Red Sea resort areas are generally safe and well set up for families, with most major resorts maintaining their own security and child-safety standards. Normal travel precautions apply, as with any destination.
What should we pack for a Red Sea family resort vacation?
High-SPF sunscreen, swim shoes for coral-lined beaches, light layers for air-conditioned spaces, snorkeling gear if your kids enjoy it, and modest cover-ups for any cultural side trips.

Final Thoughts: Choosing the Right Family Resort

The "best" resort ultimately depends on your kids' ages and what kind of trip you're after — a slide-filled waterpark resort, a quiet reef-side retreat in Marsa Alam, or a touch of palatial luxury in Sahl Hasheesh. Whichever you choose, the real magic of an Egypt family vacation is how easily a relaxed beach stay can be paired with a once-in-a-lifetime cultural day at the Pyramids.
