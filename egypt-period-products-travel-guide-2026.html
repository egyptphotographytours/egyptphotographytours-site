Chapter 1: Period Product Availability in Egypt - What's Available Where

Understanding product availability prevents stress and ensures you pack appropriately. These verified findings reflect 2026 market conditions across Egypt's major destinations.

🧴

Pads (Sanitary Towels)

Availability: Widely Available
Pharmacies, supermarkets, convenience stores

Brands found: Always, Always Maxi, Libresse, local Egyptian brands
Locations: All major cities (Cairo, Luxor, Aswan, Alexandria); tourist areas; hotel shops
Price range: 30-80 EGP ($1-2.50 USD) per pack
Tip: Bring your preferred brand if specific type needed; local options work well for most travelers

🔹

Tampons

Availability: Limited
International pharmacies, upscale supermarkets, hotel shops

Brands found: Tampax, o.b., occasionally Always
Locations: Cairo (Zamalek, Maadi), Luxor (East Bank hotels), Aswan (resort areas); rare in local markets
Price range: 60-120 EGP ($2-4 USD) per pack
Tip: Pack entire trip supply + 20% extra; don't rely on local availability

🥤

Menstrual Cups

Availability: Very Limited
Online orders only (Jumia, Amazon Egypt)

Brands found: Rarely in physical stores; DivaCup, Lena occasionally via online retailers
Delivery time: 3-7 days for online orders; not reliable for travel needs
Price range: 300-600 EGP ($10-20 USD) if found
Tip: Bring your cup from home; pack sterilization supplies (boiling water access)

🌿

Eco-Friendly Options

Availability: Not Available Locally
Bring from home

Reusable cloth pads: Virtually unavailable in Egypt
Period underwear: Not sold in Egyptian retail
Biodegradable disposables: Limited selection; bring preferred brands
Tip: Pack eco-products from home; plan for discreet disposal when eco-options unavailable

Universal Disposal Best Practices

  • Never flush period products: Egyptian plumbing isn't designed for tampons/pads; causes blockages
  • Wrap securely: Use toilet paper or provided wrappers; seal in zip-lock bag for transport if needed
  • Carry disposal supplies: Small zip-lock bags, biodegradable wipes, hand sanitizer in daypack
  • Dispose promptly: Don't carry used products longer than necessary; find appropriate disposal at next opportunity
  • Respect local norms: Follow facility protocols; ask staff discreetly if unsure about disposal options

Discreet Disposal Kit Essentials

  • Small zip-lock bags: For sealing used products when proper disposal unavailable
  • Biodegradable wipes: For cleaning hands/surfaces; eco-friendly option
  • Hand sanitizer: Essential when soap/water unavailable
  • Extra toilet paper: For wrapping and facility preparedness
  • Opaque pouch: For carrying supplies discreetly in daypack

Chapter 3: Cultural Sensitivity Around Menstruation in Egypt

Understanding cultural context prevents awkwardness and shows respect for Egyptian social norms. These guidelines enable confident, culturally-appropriate period management.

Cultural Context & Communication Guidelines

Situation Cultural Context Appropriate Approach Phrases to Use
Asking for products at pharmacy Pharmacy staff accustomed to requests; discretion appreciated Use discreet language; point to product if unsure of Arabic term "Sanitary products" or "women's products" vs. explicit terms

Cultural Sensitivity Best Practices

  • Use discreet language: "Sanitary products" or "women's products" vs. explicit terms in public
  • Respect privacy norms: Avoid public discussions; keep period management private
  • Understand religious context: Some mosques restrict menstruating women from prayer areas; research site policies
  • Trust professional staff: Hotel concierge, tour guides, and pharmacy staff handle requests professionally
  • Carry discreet packaging: Opaque pouches prevent accidental exposure in public spaces

Religious Site Considerations

Mosque visits: Some mosques restrict menstruating women from prayer areas; research policies before visiting. Most tourist mosques (Muhammad Ali, Al-Azhar) are accommodating to international visitors.
Temple sites: Ancient Egyptian sites have no menstruation restrictions; focus on practical facility access.
Discretion tip: If unsure about site policies, ask tour guide or site staff privately: "Are there any restrictions for women visitors today?" Egypt Photography Tours provides site-specific guidance in pre-trip briefings.

Packing Strategy by Trip Length

  • 3-5 day trip: Pack primary products for entire trip + small backup supply; minimal disposal supplies
  • 6-10 day trip: Pack primary products + full backup supply; comprehensive disposal kit; comfort items
  • 11+ day trip: Pack primary products + full backup; research local pharmacy locations; plan for mid-trip resupply if needed
  • Multi-destination trip: Distribute supplies across luggage; keep emergency kit in daypack; research product availability at each destination

Climate-Specific Packing Tips

  • Heat management: Egypt's climate may increase discomfort; pack breathable fabrics, stay hydrated, consider lighter-flow products
  • Dust protection: Desert environments can affect product comfort; pack extra wipes for cleaning
  • Water activities: If swimming, pack tampons/cup + backup pads; research facility access at beaches/resorts
  • Photography considerations: Dark clothing helps hide potential leaks during outdoor shoots; pack spare outfit for important photo sessions
