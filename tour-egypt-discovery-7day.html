Detailed Itinerary: 7-Day Egypt Discovery Tour

Day 1: Arrival in Cairo - Welcome to Egypt
Welcome to Egypt!

Upon arrival at Cairo International Airport, meet your tour representative for assistance with luggage and customs procedures. Enjoy a VIP fast-track service through immigration (available upon request).

Private Transfer to Your Hotel

Private air-conditioned vehicle transfer to your selected hotel in Cairo. During the transfer, your guide provides an initial orientation to Cairo's geography, neighborhoods, and practical travel tips for your stay.

Hotel Check-in & Evening at Leisure

Check into your hotel and settle in. Evening at leisure to relax after your flight. Optional activities: Short walking tour of downtown Cairo to experience the city's vibrant atmosphere, Nile dinner cruise with traditional entertainment, or sound and light show at the pyramids. Overnight stay in Cairo.

Photography Opportunity

Optional evening photography session capturing Cairo's skyline from strategic viewpoints. Learn basic night photography techniques from our professional photographer.

Day 2: Giza Pyramids, Sphinx & Egyptian Museum
Morning: Giza Plateau Exploration

After breakfast at your hotel, begin your day at the Giza Plateau, home to the Great Pyramid of Khufu (Cheops), Pyramid of Khafre, and Pyramid of Menkaure – the last surviving wonder of the Seven Wonders of the Ancient World. Your Egyptologist guide provides detailed explanations of pyramid construction, historical context, and recent archaeological discoveries.

Great Sphinx & Valley Temple

Explore the enigmatic Great Sphinx of Giza, the largest monolithic statue in the world. Visit the Valley Temple where ancient mummification rituals were performed. Learn about the different theories surrounding the Sphinx's construction and purpose.

Professional Photoshoot Session

Professional photography session at optimal locations around the pyramids. Our photographer knows the best angles, lighting conditions, and timing for stunning pyramid photos. Optional camel or horseback ride with stunning panoramic views of the pyramids (additional cost).

Afternoon: Egyptian Museum Visit

Afternoon visit to the Egyptian Museum in Tahrir Square (or the new Grand Egyptian Museum if open). Explore the world's most extensive collection of Pharaonic antiquities, including the golden treasures of King Tutankhamun. Your guide highlights key artifacts and provides historical context. Evening transfer back to hotel in Cairo.

Day 3: Islamic & Coptic Cairo Tour
Morning: Citadel of Salah El-Din

Morning visit to the Citadel of Salah El-Din, a medieval Islamic fortress offering breathtaking views over Cairo. Explore the impressive Mosque of Muhammad Ali (Alabaster Mosque) with its Ottoman architecture. Learn about Islamic Cairo's history and architectural evolution.

Coptic Cairo Exploration

Continue to Coptic Cairo to see the Hanging Church (El Muallaqa), one of Egypt's oldest churches built in the 3rd century. Visit Abu Serga Church (St. Sergius), built above a cave believed to have sheltered the Holy Family during their flight to Egypt. Explore the historic Ben Ezra Synagogue, Cairo's oldest Jewish temple.

Architectural Photography Session

Professional photography focusing on Islamic and Coptic architectural details. Learn techniques for capturing intricate designs, patterns, and interior spaces with challenging lighting conditions.

Afternoon: Khan El Khalili Bazaar

Afternoon stroll through Khan El Khalili Bazaar, Cairo's most famous souk dating back to the 14th century. Explore narrow alleyways filled with spices, lamps, souvenirs, jewelry, and local crafts. Enjoy a traditional Egyptian tea or coffee at the historic El Fishawy Café, frequented by writers and artists for centuries. Overnight stay in Cairo.

Day 4: Alexandria Day Trip - Mediterranean Egypt
Morning Travel to Alexandria

Early morning transfer by private car to Alexandria, Egypt's second-largest city and a jewel of the Mediterranean. The approximately 3-hour journey includes commentary about Egypt's northern coast and Alexandria's historical significance.

Catacombs of Kom El Shoqafa

Visit the Catacombs of Kom El Shoqafa, one of the Seven Wonders of the Middle Ages. This Roman burial site blends Pharaonic, Roman, and Greek artistic styles. Explore three levels of tombs, statues, and intricate carvings.

Qaitbay Citadel & Bibliotheca Alexandrina

Explore the impressive Qaitbay Citadel, built in the 15th century on the exact site of the legendary Lighthouse of Alexandria (Pharos), one of the original Seven Wonders. Visit the modern Bibliotheca Alexandrina, a revival of the ancient Library of Alexandria with stunning architecture and extensive collections.

Coastal Photography Session

Photography session along the Mediterranean coast. Capture Alexandria's unique blend of ancient and modern architecture against the sea backdrop. Learn techniques for coastal and architectural photography.

Lunch & Return to Cairo

Enjoy fresh seafood lunch at a local restaurant overlooking the Mediterranean. Stroll along the Corniche for final views of Alexandria's coastline. Evening return to Cairo for overnight stay.

Day 5: Luxor West Bank - Valley of the Kings
Morning Transfer to Luxor

Morning domestic flight from Cairo to Luxor (approximately 1 hour). Upon arrival, meet your local Luxor guide and transfer to your hotel. Luxor is often called the world's greatest open-air museum.

Valley of the Kings

Cross the Nile to the West Bank of Luxor. Explore the Valley of the Kings, where pharaohs including Tutankhamun, Ramses II, and Seti I were buried in richly decorated tombs. Visit 3-4 tombs (selection based on preservation and availability) with detailed explanations of hieroglyphics, burial practices, and tomb architecture.

Temple of Queen Hatshepsut

Visit the magnificent Temple of Queen Hatshepsut at Deir el-Bahari, one of Egypt's most striking temples built into the cliffs. Learn about Egypt's female pharaoh and the temple's unique architectural design.

Colossi of Memnon

Stop at the Colossi of Memnon, two massive stone statues of Pharaoh Amenhotep III that have stood for 3,400 years. Learn about their history and the ancient "singing" phenomenon.

Tomb Photography Session

Professional photography session focusing on tomb art and temple reliefs. Learn techniques for capturing detailed hieroglyphics and wall paintings in low-light conditions without flash.

Evening Options

Evening optional Sound & Light Show at Karnak Temple or relaxing dinner at a local restaurant. Overnight stay in Luxor.

Day 6: Luxor East Bank - Karnak & Luxor Temples
Morning: Karnak Temple Complex

Visit the awe-inspiring Karnak Temple Complex, the largest religious structure ever built, dedicated to the god Amun-Ra. Walk along the Great Hypostyle Hall with its 134 massive columns. Explore the sacred lake, obelisks, and various temples within the complex. Your guide explains the significance of each section and the temple's evolution over 2,000 years.

Luxor Temple

Continue to Luxor Temple, beautifully illuminated if visited around sunset. This temple was originally connected to Karnak by the Avenue of Sphinxes. Explore the temple's various sections from the New Kingdom through Roman periods, including a Christian church and mosque built within the temple complex.

Temple Photography Masterclass

Extended photography session at Karnak and Luxor temples. Focus on capturing scale, architectural details, and the play of light through columns. Learn composition techniques for grand structures.

Afternoon Leisure & Optional Activities

Afternoon at leisure with optional activities: Felucca sailboat ride on the Nile River at sunset, visit to Luxor Museum, shopping in local markets, or relaxing by the hotel pool. Evening at leisure in Luxor.

Day 7: Return to Cairo & Departure
Morning Transfer to Cairo

Morning domestic flight from Luxor back to Cairo. Depending on your international flight schedule, we optimize timing for comfort and convenience.

Free Time & Last-minute Activities

Free time in Cairo for last-minute shopping, quick visits to additional sites based on your interests, or relaxing before departure. Options include: Islamic Cairo mosques, Coptic Museum, local markets for final souvenirs, or traditional Egyptian lunch.

Final Photography Review

Review of best photos taken during the tour. Basic editing tips and guidance on selecting images for sharing and printing. Discussion of photography techniques learned during the tour.

Departure Transfer

Transfer to Cairo International Airport for your outbound flight. Assistance with check-in and departure procedures. Farewell from your guide and photographer.

Post-Tour Services

Within 7 days of tour completion: Delivery of edited high-resolution photos via digital download. Follow-up email with additional Egypt travel tips and resources. Invitation to join our traveler community for future travel inspiration.

Understanding Egyptian Culture: Beyond Surface Level

Egypt is more than ancient monuments - it's a living civilization with rich traditions, complex social dynamics, and warm hospitality. Our tours help you understand:

Modern Egyptian Society

Learn about contemporary Egyptian life: family structures, education systems, economic realities, and cultural values. Understanding modern Egypt makes ancient sites more meaningful by showing historical continuity.

Religious Diversity

Egypt is predominantly Muslim with a significant Christian minority (Copts). We explain religious practices, holidays, and how religious identity shapes Egyptian culture and politics.

Culinary Traditions

Egyptian cuisine reflects the country's agricultural abundance and historical influences. We introduce you to authentic Egyptian foods in appropriate settings, from street food stalls to family restaurants.

Social Customs

Learn appropriate greetings, gift-giving customs, negotiation styles in markets, and social norms that help you interact respectfully with Egyptians.
