Egypt Itinerary 7 Days: The Perfect Route for First-Timers

Unlock the magic of Egypt in one week: from the Pyramids of Giza to the temples of Luxor and Aswan. This expertly crafted route balances iconic sights, cultural immersion, and photography magic — with zero stress.

March 1, 2026 Cairo, Luxor, Aswan 7-Day Itinerary, First-Timers, Egypt Travel 65 min read

Seven days. One majestic country. Countless first-time visitors ask: “Can I really see the best of Egypt in a week?” The answer is a resounding yes — provided you follow a smart, logical route that connects the absolute highlights without exhausting you. This guide unveils the perfect 7-day Egypt itinerary, designed exclusively for first-timers by Egypt Photography Tours. We’ll weave in photography hot spots, cultural etiquette, and seamless logistics — and show you how our private tours elevate every moment.

Why this itinerary works

It combines the monumental pyramids of Giza, the vibrant chaos of Cairo, the ancient wonders of Luxor, and the serene beauty of a Nile cruise — all in one week. You'll fly between cities to maximise time, and enjoy a 3-night Nile cruise to unpack the temples of Edfu and Kom Ombo. It's the classic "greatest hits" route, polished to perfection.

Day 1: Arrival in Cairo – Giza Pyramids & Sphinx

Your Egyptian adventure begins at Cairo International Airport. After a warm meet-and-greet, your private guide and driver will whisk you to the legendary Giza Plateau. Here, the last remaining Wonder of the Ancient World awaits.

Morning: The Pyramids of Giza

Stand in awe before the Great Pyramid of Khufu, Khafre, and Menkaure. Your Egyptologist guide will decode the mysteries of their construction, and you'll have ample time for that iconic “sphinx kiss” photo. For photographers, the light is magical in the morning.

Afternoon: Solar Boat Museum & Panoramic Viewpoint

Visit the remarkably preserved Khufu ship, then drive to the panoramic viewpoint for a sweeping shot of all three pyramids. Optional camel ride for a timeless snapshot.

Evening: Sound & Light Show (optional)

After dinner at a local restaurant, you may choose to attend the Sound & Light show at Giza — a dramatic retelling of ancient history.

Photography tip: Golden hour (just before sunset) paints the pyramids in warm hues. Our Private Pyramids Photography Tour ensures you're at the best spots at the right time, with a guide who knows every angle.

Overnight: Cairo (Mena House or similar)

Day 2: Cairo – Egyptian Museum, Citadel & Khan el-Khalili

Morning: Egyptian Museum (Tahrir)

Dive into 5,000 years of history at the Egyptian Museum, home to Tutankhamun’s golden mask and the treasures of the royal mummies. Your guide brings the artifacts to life with stories of pharaohs and archaeologists.

Afternoon: Salah El-Din Citadel & Mohamed Ali Mosque

Perched on a hill, the Citadel offers panoramic views of Cairo. The alabaster mosque of Mohamed Ali is a masterpiece of Ottoman architecture — a must for interior photography.

Evening: Khan el-Khalili Bazaar

Lose yourself in the labyrinthine alleys of Cairo's most famous souk. Sip mint tea at El Fishawy café, practice your bargaining skills, and soak up the atmosphere.

Our recommendation: Combine Days 1–2 seamlessly with our 2-Day Immersive Cairo & Giza Tour, which includes expert guiding and all transfers.

Day 3: Fly to Luxor – East Bank (Karnak & Luxor Temple)

A short morning flight brings you to Luxor, the world’s greatest open-air museum. After checking into your Nile cruise ship (or hotel), you’ll explore the East Bank.

Karnak Temple

Karnak is not just a temple; it’s a sprawling complex of colossal columns, obelisks, and sacred lakes. The Hypostyle Hall with its 134 giant pillars is a photographer’s dream — play with light and shadow.

Luxor Temple at Dusk

As the sun sets, Luxor Temple is beautifully illuminated. Stroll along the avenue of sphinxes and capture the illuminated pylon.

Insider tip: Most Nile cruises include lunch and dinner on board. You’ll sail overnight to Edfu.

Day 4: Luxor West Bank – Valley of the Kings, Hatshepsut & Colossi of Memnon

Early Morning: Valley of the Kings

Cross the Nile to the West Bank, land of the dead. Enter tombs of pharaohs, including the famous tomb of Tutankhamun (optional extra). The vibrant wall paintings are breathtaking.

Mid-morning: Temple of Hatshepsut at Deir el-Bahri

The striking terraced temple of Egypt’s only female pharaoh rises against the cliffs. Best photographed in the morning light.

Afternoon: Colossi of Memnon & Optional Hot Air Balloon

See the two massive stone statues of Amenhotep III, then optional hot air balloon flight over the West Bank at sunrise (book ahead).

Your cruise continues sailing towards Edfu.

Day 5: Nile Cruise – Edfu & Kom Ombo Temples

Morning: Edfu Temple

Dock at Edfu and take a horse-drawn carriage to the best-preserved temple in Egypt, dedicated to Horus. The colossal gateway and granite statues are stunning.

Afternoon: Kom Ombo Temple

Unique double temple dedicated to Sobek (crocodile god) and Horus the Elder. Don’t miss the crocodile mummies in the adjoining chapel. The setting on the Nile bank is perfect for sunset photos.

Sail onwards to Aswan while enjoying a Nubian folk show on board.

Day 6: Aswan – Philae Temple, High Dam & Felucca Ride

Morning: Philae Temple

Take a motorboat to the island temple of Philae, dedicated to goddess Isis. The ethereal setting and Ptolemaic carvings are unforgettable.

Afternoon: High Dam & Unfinished Obelisk

Visit the modern marvel of the High Dam, then see the massive Unfinished Obelisk, which would have been the largest ever.

Evening: Felucca Ride on the Nile

Relax on a traditional Egyptian sailboat as the sun dips behind the dunes. Sip tea and absorb the tranquility.

Optional add-on: Early morning flight to Abu Simbel (ask us about arranging it).

Day 7: Fly Back to Cairo & Departure

After breakfast, fly from Aswan to Cairo. Depending on your flight time, you might squeeze in last-minute souvenir shopping or visit a hidden gem like the new Grand Egyptian Museum (partial opening). Your driver will then transfer you to Cairo International Airport for your departure, filled with a thousand memories.

Pro tip: If you have a late flight, consider our Alexandria Day Trip as an extension, but for a 7-day itinerary, we recommend resting or exploring Coptic Cairo.

Essential Tips for Your 7-Day Egypt Adventure

  • Best time to go: October to April for mild weather. Avoid summer (June–August) unless you love intense heat.
  • Packing essentials: Lightweight linen, comfortable walking shoes, scarf for temples, sunscreen, hat, power bank, and a good camera with wide-angle lens.
  • Currency & tipping: Carry Egyptian pounds for small purchases and tipping. See our detailed Baksheesh Guide for exact amounts.
  • Stay hydrated: Drink bottled water only, and lots of it.
  • Dress modestly: Shoulders and knees covered when entering mosques and temples.

Frequently Asked Questions

Q: Can I add Abu Simbel to this 7-day itinerary? A: Yes, but it requires a very early flight from Aswan on Day 6 or an extra day. We can arrange it as an optional add-on — contact us.

Q: Is a Nile cruise worth it for 3 nights? A: Absolutely. It’s a floating hotel, and you unpack only once while covering multiple temples. Plus, the river views are magical.

Q: Do I need a visa? A: Most nationalities can get a tourist visa on arrival at Cairo airport ($25 USD) or e-visa online.
