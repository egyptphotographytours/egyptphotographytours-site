Egypt Itinerary 7 Days: The Perfect Route

Unlock the magic of Egypt in one week: from the Pyramids of Giza to the temples of Luxor and Aswan. This expertly crafted route balances iconic sights, cultural immersion, and photography magic — with zero stress.

Home/ Resources/ Egypt Itinerary 7 Days
Updated June 20, 2026 35 min read Travel Itinerary By Hossam, Egyptologist & Photographer

Seven days. One majestic country. Countless first-time visitors ask: “Can I really see the best of Egypt in a week?” The answer is a resounding yes — provided you follow a smart, logical route that connects the absolute highlights without exhausting you. Before you book your flights, ensure you have the right travel documents by checking our Egypt Visa Guide and prepare for your trip with our Ultimate Packing List.

This guide unveils the perfect 7-day Egypt itinerary, designed exclusively for first-timers by Egypt Photography Tours. We’ll weave in photography hot spots, cultural etiquette, and seamless logistics — and show you how our private tours elevate every moment. For insights on the best weather for your trip, read our Egypt Weather Guide.

Why This Itinerary Works

It combines the monumental pyramids of Giza, the vibrant chaos of Cairo, the ancient wonders of Luxor, and the serene beauty of a Nile cruise — all in one week. You'll fly between cities to maximise time, and enjoy a 3-night Nile cruise to unpack the temples of Edfu and Kom Ombo. It's the classic "greatest hits" route, polished to perfection. Day 1: Arrival in Cairo – Giza Pyramids & Sphinx

Your Egyptian adventure begins at Cairo International Airport. After a warm meet-and-greet, your private guide and driver will whisk you to the legendary Giza Plateau. Here, the last remaining Wonder of the Ancient World awaits.

  • Morning/Afternoon: Stand in awe before the Great Pyramid of Khufu, Khafre, and Menkaure. Your Egyptologist guide will decode the mysteries of their construction, and you'll have ample time for that iconic “sphinx kiss” photo.
  • Panoramic Viewpoint: Drive to the panoramic viewpoint for a sweeping shot of all three pyramids. Optional camel ride for a timeless snapshot.
  • Evening: After dinner at a local restaurant, you may choose to attend the Sound & Light show at Giza — a dramatic retelling of ancient history.

Photography tip: Golden hour (just before sunset) paints the pyramids in warm hues. Our Private Pyramids Photography Tour ensures you're at the best spots at the right time, with a guide who knows every angle.

Overnight: Cairo (Mena House or similar)

Day 2: Cairo – Egyptian Museum, Citadel & Khan el-Khalili

  • Morning: Dive into 5,000 years of history at the Egyptian Museum, home to Tutankhamun’s golden mask and the treasures of the royal mummies.
  • Afternoon: Perched on a hill, the Salah El-Din Citadel offers panoramic views of Cairo. The alabaster mosque of Mohamed Ali is a masterpiece of Ottoman architecture.
  • Evening: Lose yourself in the labyrinthine alleys of Cairo's most famous souk, Khan el-Khalili. Sip mint tea at El Fishawy café and practice your bargaining skills. For a complete guide to the bazaar, read our Khan el-Khalili Bazaar Guide.

Our recommendation: Combine Days 1–2 seamlessly with our 2-Day Immersive Cairo & Giza Tour, which includes expert guiding and all transfers.

Day 3: Fly to Luxor – East Bank (Karnak & Luxor Temple)

A short morning flight brings you to Luxor, the world’s greatest open-air museum. After checking into your Nile cruise ship, you’ll explore the East Bank.

  • Karnak Temple: A sprawling complex of colossal columns, obelisks, and sacred lakes. The Hypostyle Hall with its 134 giant pillars is a photographer’s dream.
  • Luxor Temple at Dusk: As the sun sets, Luxor Temple is beautifully illuminated. Stroll along the avenue of sphinxes and capture the illuminated pylon.

Insider tip: Most Nile cruises include lunch and dinner on board. You’ll sail overnight to Edfu.

Day 4: Luxor West Bank – Valley of the Kings & Hatshepsut

  • Early Morning: Cross the Nile to the West Bank, land of the dead. Enter tombs of pharaohs in the Valley of the Kings, including the famous tomb of Tutankhamun.
  • Mid-morning: The striking terraced Temple of Hatshepsut at Deir el-Bahri rises against the cliffs. Best photographed in the morning light.
  • Afternoon: See the two massive stone statues of the Colossi of Memnon, then optional hot air balloon flight over the West Bank at sunrise.

Your cruise continues sailing towards Edfu.

Day 5: Nile Cruise – Edfu & Kom Ombo Temples

  • Morning: Dock at Edfu and take a horse-drawn carriage to the best-preserved temple in Egypt, dedicated to Horus.
  • Afternoon: Visit the unique double temple of Kom Ombo, dedicated to Sobek (crocodile god) and Horus the Elder. Don’t miss the crocodile mummies in the adjoining chapel. For a detailed guide to these temples, see our Edfu & Kom Ombo Temples Guide.

Sail onwards to Aswan while enjoying a Nubian folk show on board.

Day 6: Aswan – Philae Temple, High Dam & Felucca Ride

  • Morning: Take a motorboat to the island temple of Philae, dedicated to goddess Isis. The ethereal setting and Ptolemaic carvings are unforgettable.
  • Afternoon: Visit the modern marvel of the High Dam, then see the massive Unfinished Obelisk.
  • Evening: Relax on a traditional Egyptian Felucca sailboat as the sun dips behind the dunes. Sip tea and absorb the tranquility.

Optional add-on: Early morning flight to Abu Simbel (ask us about arranging it).

Day 7: Fly Back to Cairo & Departure

After breakfast, fly from Aswan to Cairo. Depending on your flight time, you might squeeze in last-minute souvenir shopping or visit a hidden gem like the new Grand Egyptian Museum. Your driver will then transfer you to Cairo International Airport for your departure, filled with a thousand memories.

Pro tip: If you have a late flight, consider our Alexandria Day Trip as an extension, but for a 7-day itinerary, we recommend resting or exploring Coptic Cairo.

Essential Tips for Your 7-Day Egypt Adventure

  • Best time to go: October to April for mild weather. Avoid summer (June–August) unless you love intense heat.
  • Packing essentials: Lightweight linen, comfortable walking shoes, scarf for temples, sunscreen, hat, power bank, and a good camera with wide-angle lens. See our Packing List for details.
  • Currency & tipping: Carry Egyptian pounds for small purchases and tipping. See our detailed Baksheesh Guide for exact amounts.
  • Stay hydrated: Drink bottled water only, and lots of it.
  • Dress modestly: Shoulders and knees covered when entering mosques and temples. Read our Egypt Dress Code Guide.

Frequently Asked Questions

Is 7 days enough for a first-time visit to Egypt?
Absolutely. A well-planned 7-day itinerary allows you to experience the highlights: Cairo's pyramids and museum, a Nile cruise between Luxor and Aswan, and the temples of Edfu and Kom Ombo. It's a perfect introduction.
What is the best 7-day Egypt itinerary for first-timers?
The classic route: Days 1-2 in Cairo (Giza, Egyptian Museum, Islamic Cairo). Day 3 fly to Luxor, visit East Bank. Day 4 West Bank, start Nile cruise. Day 5 sail to Edfu & Kom Ombo. Day 6 Aswan (Philae, High Dam). Day 7 fly back to Cairo for departure.
Do I need a tour for a 7-day Egypt trip?
While independent travel is possible, a guided tour enriches your experience with deep historical context, skip-the-line access, and stress-free logistics. Our 7-Day Egypt Discovery Tour handles every detail, including expert Egyptologist guides.
What should I pack for a 7-day Egypt itinerary?
Lightweight, modest clothing (cotton/Linen), comfortable walking shoes, sun hat, sunglasses, sunscreen, a scarf for temples, a light jacket for winter evenings, and your camera. For the cruise, one smart outfit for dinners.
Can I add Abu Simbel to a 7-day itinerary?
Yes, but it requires an early morning flight from Aswan or a long drive. We recommend an optional add-on to our 7-day tour; we can arrange a pre-sunrise flight to Abu Simbel and return to Aswan in time for the rest of the itinerary.
