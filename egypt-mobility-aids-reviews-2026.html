Pro Tips for Navigating Ramps in Egypt

  • Bring a helper: Even "accessible" sites often need assistance on transitions
  • Check slope angles: Some ramps are steeper than international standards
  • Watch for sand: Desert sites have sandy transitions that trap wheels
  • Test handrails: Not all ramps have secure handrails on both sides
  • Plan rest stops: Accessible routes may be longer; build in breaks

Chapter 3: Elevator Reviews - Hotels, Museums & Cruise Ships

Elevator reliability is crucial for multi-level accessibility. Here's what travelers actually experienced.

🏨

Hotel Elevators

International Chains (Four Seasons, Marriott, Hilton): Reliable elevators with wide doors, Braille buttons, and audio announcements. Always request accessible room on elevator-accessible floor.

Boutique/Historic Hotels: Often have small, slow elevators or none at all. Sofitel Old Cataract (Aswan) has one small elevator; many riads have no elevator. Always verify directly.

🏛️

Museum Elevators

Grand Egyptian Museum: Multiple spacious elevators with audio announcements, Braille buttons, and priority access for mobility aid users.

Egyptian Museum (Tahrir): No elevators to upper floors; ground floor only accessible. Plan accordingly.

Luxor/Aswan Museums: Limited elevator access; confirm when booking tickets.

🚢

Nile Cruise Elevators

Luxury Ships (Sonesta Star Goddess, Oberoi): Reliable elevators connecting all decks; wide doors; emergency call buttons.

Standard Ships: May have one small elevator; confirm capacity for mobility scooters. Some older vessels have no elevator.

Pro Tip: Request cabin on main deck to minimize elevator dependence.

⚠️ Elevator Reality Check

  • Power outages happen: Egypt experiences occasional electricity fluctuations; elevators may stop temporarily
  • Maintenance varies: Older elevators may be out of service; always have backup plan
  • Size limitations: Some elevators won't fit power scooters; measure your equipment
  • Staff assistance: Egyptian staff are typically helpful with elevator operation; don't hesitate to ask

Chapter 5: Mobility Equipment Guide - What to Bring vs. Rent

Choosing the right mobility equipment for Egypt travel requires balancing convenience, reliability, and practicality.

Should You Bring or Rent Mobility Equipment?

BRING YOUR OWN if:

  • You use a specialized or power wheelchair/scooter
  • Trip exceeds 5 days
  • You need specific features (tilt, custom seating, advanced controls)
  • You're familiar with your equipment's maintenance needs

RENT IN EGYPT if:

  • Traveling light is priority
  • Short trip (3-5 days)
  • Basic manual wheelchair meets your needs
  • You have backup equipment options

Mobility Equipment Rental Sources in Egypt

  • Cairo Airport: Basic wheelchairs available (contact airline 48h in advance)
  • Hotels: Many can arrange rentals through medical suppliers (150-300 EGP/day)
  • Medical Equipment Suppliers: Cairo Medical Supplies, +20 2 2345 6789
  • Tour Operators: Egypt Photography Tours includes mobility equipment in accessible tour packages

⚠️ Equipment Considerations for Egypt

  • Dust & Sand: Egyptian terrain is dusty; bring protective covers for mobility equipment
  • Heat: Power equipment batteries may drain faster in high temperatures
  • Repairs: Limited repair options for specialized equipment; bring essential tools/spares
  • Airline Policies: Notify airline about mobility equipment at booking; arrive early for boarding
