Egypt Visa Types: Which One Do You Need?

1. Egypt Tourist Visa (Most Common) Recommended for Tourists

The Egypt tourist visa is designed for visitors coming for tourism, sightseeing, family visits, or short-term photography expeditions. It's available in several formats:

Tourist Visa Options:

  • Single-entry tourist visa: Valid for 90 days from issue, allows one entry, maximum stay 30 days
  • Multiple-entry tourist visa: Valid for 90 days from issue, allows multiple entries, maximum stay 30 days per entry
  • Visa extension: Can be extended in Egypt for additional 3 months

Best for: Most Egypt Photography Tours participants, holiday travelers, photographers, first-time visitors

2. Egypt Business Visa

Required for travelers engaging in business activities, attending conferences, or meeting with Egyptian companies.

Requirements:

  • Invitation letter from Egyptian company
  • Business cover letter from employer
  • Proof of business activities
  • Must be obtained from Egyptian embassy/consulate

Processing time: 5-10 business days at embassy

3. Egypt Transit Visa

For travelers passing through Egypt to another destination, with a layover exceeding 6 hours.

Requirements:

  • Valid onward ticket within 72 hours
  • Visa for final destination (if required)
  • Can be obtained at airport for short transits

Note: Most international airports offer transit areas; check if you need to clear immigration.

Egypt Visa Application Methods: 2026 Guide

1

Egypt e-Visa (Electronic Visa) Most Recommended

The Egypt e-Visa is the most convenient option for most travelers. Applied for online before travel, it provides guaranteed entry approval and avoids airport queues.

e-Visa Application Process:

  1. Visit the official Egypt e-Visa portal (visa2egypt.gov.eg)
  2. Create an account and complete the online application form
  3. Upload required documents (passport scan, photo)
  4. Pay the visa fee online ($25 single entry, $60 multiple entry)
  5. Receive e-Visa approval via email within 3-7 business days
  6. Print the e-Visa and present it with your passport at immigration

Processing Time: Standard 3-7 business days • Expedited 1-3 business days (extra fee)

Validity: 90 days from issue date • Stay: Up to 30 days per entry

2

Visa on Arrival (VOA)

Available at major Egyptian international airports for eligible nationalities. While convenient, it has risks including possible denial and long queues.

VOA Process:

  1. Arrive at Egyptian airport (Cairo, Luxor, Hurghada, Sharm El-Sheikh, etc.)
  2. Proceed to Visa on Arrival counter before immigration
  3. Pay $25 USD cash (exact amount recommended)
  4. Receive visa sticker to place in passport
  5. Proceed to immigration with completed arrival card

Requirements: Passport valid 6+ months • $25 USD cash • Return/onward ticket • Accommodation proof

Risk: Possible denial if documents incomplete • Long queues during peak seasons

3

Embassy/Consulate Visa

Required for special cases, longer stays, or nationals from countries not eligible for e-Visa or VOA.

Embassy Application Process:

  1. Locate nearest Egyptian embassy or consulate
  2. Download and complete visa application form
  3. Prepare required documents (varies by visa type)
  4. Submit application in person or via authorized agent
  5. Pay visa fee (varies by nationality and visa type)
  6. Collect passport with visa stamp (typically 5-10 business days)

Best for: Long-term stays • Business/work visas • Special circumstances

Expert Tips for Egypt Visa Success 2026

Proven Strategies for Smooth Visa Processing

Timing is Everything:

  • Apply early but not too early: e-Visas are valid 90 days from issue—time your application 3-4 weeks before travel
  • Avoid peak seasons: December-January and March-April see longer processing times
  • Weekday applications: Submit early in the week (Tuesday-Thursday) for fastest processing

Document Preparation:

  • High-quality scans: Ensure passport scans are clear, all edges visible, and file size under 2MB
  • Accurate information: Double-check all details match your passport exactly
  • Consistent documents: Hotel bookings and flight itineraries should match visa application dates

Avoid Common Mistakes:

  • ❌ Applying with passport expiring within 6 months
  • ❌ Using blurry or incorrect passport photos
  • ❌ Providing inconsistent travel dates
  • ❌ Waiting until last minute for embassy visas

Visa Denials: Causes and Prevention

While Egypt visa denials are rare for straightforward tourism, they can occur. Common reasons include:

Primary Denial Reasons:

  • Incomplete documentation - Missing required documents or information
  • Passport issues - Insufficient validity, damage, or suspicious alterations
  • Security concerns - Name matches on watchlists (rare but possible)
  • Previous immigration violations - Overstays or violations in Egypt or other countries

Prevention Strategies:

  • ✅ Use Egypt Photography Tours visa guidance services
  • ✅ Apply through official channels only (avoid third-party scams)
  • ✅ Ensure all documents are complete and accurate
  • ✅ Apply with sufficient lead time to address any issues
